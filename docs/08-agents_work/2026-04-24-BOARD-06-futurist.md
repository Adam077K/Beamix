Reviewer persona: The Futurist. No technical, budget, or timeline limits. Eighteen-month horizon. Input: v2 design direction (good, tame). Mandate: dream bigger. Paint Beamix in 2027 if we choose courage.

Date: 2026-04-24
Lens: computer-use agents, generative UI, spatial computing (Vision Pro, Quest 3, Meta Ray-Bans), voice-first (Humane, Rabbit, OpenAI Advanced Voice), memory-based AI (Letta, Mem0, ChatGPT memory), shareable AI artifacts (Perplexity pages, ChatGPT share links, v0 generations), character-driven product (Arc Max weather, Notion AI, tldraw fairies, Linear Rauno's prototypes), emotional UI (Apple Intelligence, Rabbit R1 flops and wins). First live AI search tool that shows its work to humans = once-a-decade category opportunity. v2 is playing it safe.

## 1. THE PITCH — BEAMIX IN 2027 (1 paragraph)

It is Tuesday at 07:41 in Tel Aviv. Yael opens her laptop, and Beamie is already in the corner of her screen — a small, familiar, warm-silver presence that has been her business partner for eight months. Before Yael can even open the app, Beamie tilts toward the upper-right: "בוקר טוב. תוך 48 שעות Perplexity ציטטה את המתחרה שלך שלוש פעמים. שלחתי את הצוות לבדוק למה — רוצה לצפות?" Yael nods. Her screen transforms — not into a dashboard but into a Stage. Seven AI engines appear as orbs around her site card, and she watches Beamie courier her brand to each one in 30 seconds of kinetic, almost-cinematic motion. A Shareable Replay auto-generates and lands in her inbox and on a `beamix.tech/yael/scan/2026-04-24` permalink she can text to her co-founder. The State of GEO Weekly — Beamix's public media property — ran a segment about her category yesterday, using anonymized data from her and 400 other SMBs; she got credit as a "contributor." At 15:00 she slips on her Vision Pro for a client call and pulls up the Spatial Stage: her site, her competitor's site, and the seven AI engines float at arms-length in 3D. She gestures, rotates, and drags her About page into Claude's orb to re-check citation depth. At 21:00, from her couch, she says "Beamie, תן לי סיכום של היום" and a voice — her voice's Hebrew — warmly narrates the day's agent wins in 11 seconds. Over eight months, Beamie has learned her language, her competitors by name, her first win (a Perplexity citation on 2025-09-12 that Beamie still calls "the Tuesday breakthrough"), her two-week vacations, her preference for short explanations, and her anxiety about a specific competitor named "Blooma." Beamie is not a tool. Beamie is staff. And Yael is now paying $499/mo happily because the alternative is firing a colleague.

## 2. THE 8-10 RADICAL IDEAS

### Idea 1 — "Beamie has a memory" — the first AI partner that remembers your story, not just your data
Day 1 Beamie is a courier. Day 90 Beamie is a colleague who knows your competitive obsession. Month 12 Beamie is staff who opens with "Yael, remember the Tuesday breakthrough? Perplexity is doing it again this week, different keyword" and pulls up the citation without being asked. Under the hood, Beamie runs a layered memory system: **Episodic** (every scan, every agent run, every user approval/rejection, timestamped — "you dismissed the schema suggestion three times between Sep and Oct, I've stopped suggesting it"), **Semantic** (a learned knowledge graph of your business — your services, your ICP, your top 5 competitors, your content voice — updated every time an agent runs), **Relational** (who you are as a user — typing speed, preferred explanation length, vocabulary, time of day you work, days you're offline), and **Narrative** (the first win, the first loss, the moments that mattered, called out by name). Beamie's inline text field starts showing "memory chips" at the top: `Remembers: your launch on Sep 12 · your competitor Blooma · your preference for Hebrew in summaries · 14 agent wins this quarter`. Users can click a memory chip to see, edit, or delete what Beamie knows — a memory control surface, not a black box.

The scenario that matters: user returns from a 3-week vacation. Beamie's first message: "ברוכה השבה. 4 דברים קרו שחשבתי שתרצי לדעת" (*welcome back, 4 things happened I thought you'd want to know*) — not a generic digest but 4 items ranked by what Beamie has learned matters to *this user specifically*: "1. Blooma got cited 9 times on Perplexity — up from 2/week. 2. Your September FAQ win got cited again on ChatGPT. 3. The agent ran the schema you finally approved and it's working. 4. A weird new engine started citing SMBs like yours — want to investigate?" This is v2 with ambition: not "here are the suggestions" but "here is what your partner noticed, ranked by what you care about."

What changes about v2: v2 says Beamie is silent by default and only appears on events. v2-courage says Beamie *curates* events through the lens of memory. v2 treats every scan the same; v2-courage treats every scan in context of 6 months of prior scans. v2 has no memory layer at all. This is the biggest unlock.

### Idea 2 — "Beamie speaks your language" — a Hebrew-native, culturally-tuned character in a global product
Israeli market first, global expansion later. Beamie's voice, micro-copy, humor, and rhythm are localized *per market* — not just translated. In Hebrew, Beamie is direct, slightly wry, uses dry Israeli humor ("רעיון כזה כזה" when scoring a Fair — not "mediocre"), references cultural shorthand ("בוא נעשה סדר" for a re-scan, "חבל על הזמן" for fast wins), uses masculine/feminine verbs correctly based on user's chosen pronouns in settings, and — critically — knows when *not* to joke (error states, billing, competitor losses stay professional). In English, Beamie is warm, plainspoken, slightly Zoom-casual. In German (Q3 2027), Beamie is precise, respectful, uses formal "Sie" until the user opts into "du." In Japanese (Q4 2027), Beamie defaults to keigo and earns casual register through relationship milestones.

The voice (both text and audio — see Idea 5) is cast per language by a human voice actor, not generic TTS. There is one Hebrew Beamie voice, one English Beamie voice, etc. They all sound related — like the same "character" translated — but each is locally credible. Beamie Hebrew might sound like Shaked Rabi or Tal Kramer (warm-wry, urban, 30s, not too young). Beamie English might sound like Jeff Bridges calibrated down 50% — textured, warm, unhurried. The UI speaks Hebrew RTL natively (not a retrofit), InterDisplay Hebrew variant, Fraunces' Arabic-compatible serif sibling for the empty state moment, and Beamie's face micro-expressions are culturally tuned — the Hebrew-market "confused" state has a different eyebrow angle (research-informed: Mediterranean expression norms differ from Anglo-Saxon).

What changes about v2: v2 treats language as i18n strings. v2-courage treats language as *identity*. The Hebrew-native Beamie is a moat that no US-first competitor will match for 2+ years. Israel becomes the beachhead, not an afterthought.

### Idea 3 — "The Shareable Scan Replay" — Beamix becomes a social object, not a SaaS login
Every scan auto-generates four shareable artifacts, ready within seconds of completion:

1. **The Permalink Replay** — `beamix.tech/s/[scan-id]` — anyone with the URL can watch the 30-second Stage animation in full fidelity (no login), see the score, see the top 3 findings. Optional "claim this scan" button converts a viewer into a signup with the scan already imported. This is Loom-for-GEO-scans.
2. **The Auto-GIF** — a 9-second silent looping GIF optimized for Twitter/X and LinkedIn: site card → 7 engines light up → score ring fills. Rendered server-side from the cached Rive animation. Branded with a small "Beamix" watermark in the corner. Users can post and tag Beamix.
3. **The Report Card Image** — a 1200×630 OG-image-sized PNG: score at 72pt, 7 engine pills with status, business name, date, "Scanned by Beamix." Pinterest-perfect. Twitter-perfect. LinkedIn-perfect.
4. **The Twitter-Ready Thread** — a one-click pre-written 4-tweet thread the user can copy or post directly: "Just ran my AI visibility scan on Beamix. Score: 47/100. ChatGPT mentions us. Perplexity doesn't. Here's what I'm fixing this week. [GIF] [permalink]"

This transforms Beamix from a B2B dashboard into a cultural object. Every user becomes a distribution channel. Every shared scan is free marketing *and* free competitive data for Beamix's State of GEO index (see Idea 6). The Stripe Press parallel: Stripe's marketing site is a brand asset; Beamix's Shareable Scan is a user asset that doubles as brand asset.

What changes about v2: v2 treats the scan as a private product event. v2-courage treats the scan as a *public social object*. Every scan is shareable by default, with a per-user privacy toggle (on by default for free-tier scans, off by default for paid tiers — a conversion incentive).

### Idea 4 — "The Spatial Stage" — Beamix on Apple Vision Pro and Quest 3 is the demo the world can't stop talking about
When the user puts on a Vision Pro, Beamix becomes spatial. The Stage is no longer a 2D modal — it is a room. The user's site card floats at arm's length in front of them. The 7 AI engines hover in a wide arc around them, at a human-scale distance (3 feet each direction). Beamie is a small, visible 3D character in the room with the user — turns its head, walks physically between engines, carries the scroll in 3D. When an engine is "thinking," its orb pulses with volumetric light. When data returns, a glowing 3D packet flies back through the room with depth and parallax.

Gestural control: pinch to select an engine, drag your About page into an engine orb to re-check it specifically, push an engine away (literally, with your hand) to deprioritize it in the scan, pull the score ring toward your face to see detail. The user can walk around the scan — see their site from behind, see the engines from above. For users without a headset, this is also the "hero demo" on the Framer marketing site: a 40-second screen-recording of the spatial Stage that goes viral on Twitter. On Quest 3, same experience. On iPhone + AR (ARKit), a simplified tabletop version — Beamix floats on the user's desk.

What changes about v2: v2 is flat-screen-first and ignores XR. v2-courage treats Vision Pro as the *hero platform* for the Stage — the spatial version is the version Beamix gets written about in TechCrunch, in Stratechery, in the MG Siegler newsletter. The 2D web app remains the daily tool. The spatial app is the cultural statement. The "Beamix sphere walk" becomes a meme.

### Idea 5 — "Talk to Beamie" — voice-first operations for busy founders
A small microphone button on Beamie, or a keyboard shortcut (Cmd+B on web, double-tap on mobile). Tap once: Beamie listens. You say, in Hebrew or English: "Beamie, run the content optimizer on my About page." Beamie's voice responds, in your language, warm and textured: "אוקיי, מתחילה. זה ייקח בערך 90 שניות. אני אודיע לך כשזה מוכן" (*okay, starting. it'll take about 90 seconds. I'll let you know when it's ready*). You close your laptop. 90 seconds later, a push notification arrives with Beamie's voice: "סיימתי. יש לי שינוי מוצע, רוצה לשמוע?" (*finished. I have a proposed change, want to hear?*)

The voice loop is OpenAI Advanced Voice-caliber (sub-500ms latency, interruptible, warm): Beamie can describe a scan, read you the top 3 findings, take dictation for a FAQ item, or narrate a completed agent's output while you're walking the dog. "Voice mode" is a distinct UI state — the screen fades to a dark stage with Beamie's face (Rive state machine: `voice-active`) at center, a subtle waveform ring around it, and real-time transcript scrolling below. Closing voice mode returns you to whatever page you were on. On mobile (primary use case), voice is the default interaction model when the phone is in landscape — the iPhone Voice Memos for GEO, as the brief suggests.

A critical voice-only feature: **"Beamie, tell me a win."** Beamie pulls from memory, picks the most significant recent agent win or score improvement, and narrates it in 15 seconds. This is a daily-affirmation feature for founders who need to feel that their investment in Beamix is paying off. It is not a gimmick — it is retention infrastructure.

What changes about v2: v2 has no voice surface at all. v2-courage makes voice the primary interaction model for mobile and a first-class option on desktop. Beamie as voice agent is the Humane-pin-we-wish-existed, the Rabbit-R1-that-works, the first voice product that does something *commercially valuable* per interaction.

### Idea 6 — "The State of GEO" — Beamix's second brand artifact, a public media property
This is Beamix's Stripe Press, Figma Config, Linear Now. A public property at `stateof.beamix.tech` (or a sibling domain) that runs on the aggregate, anonymized data from every Beamix scan. It has four surfaces:

1. **The Live AI Visibility Index** — a public leaderboard of every domain Beamix has scanned, with their anonymized industry, AI visibility score, and trend. Users can opt-in to be listed by name (most do — status signal). The index updates in real time. Journalists cite it. The index has a beautiful data-viz aesthetic — Rauno-level craft, live-updating, filterable by industry and geography.
2. **The Weekly State of GEO Newsletter** — a Tuesday-morning 800-word email written by AI from the week's aggregate scan data, with 1 hot-take from a real human editor, 1 guest contribution from a Beamix power-user (they get co-credit and a deep-link to their scan), and 3 "trending in AI search" data points nobody else has. Distributed free. 50K subscribers by Q4 2027 goal. Every email is a Beamix-branded cultural artifact.
3. **The Public Agent Gallery** — a showcase of every agent Beamix has built. Each agent has a public page (like `beamix.tech/agents/faq-builder`) with: what it does, real output examples from anonymized customers, a demo, and a "try this agent free" CTA. Makes Beamix's agent roster a visible, brandable portfolio, not a feature list.
4. **The Scan Annotations Feed** — anonymized, beautifully-presented "here is a before/after" of a real agent fix. Weekly. Instagram-optimized carousel. TikTok-optimized vertical video. The brand's constantly-refreshing top-of-funnel content engine.

What changes about v2: v2 has no public media property. v2-courage makes Beamix a brand that exists *outside the login wall*. The State of GEO becomes the TechCrunch of AI search — and Beamix owns the category by owning the category-defining publication.

### Idea 7 — "Beamie for Agencies" — the multi-client Beamie + the Meta-Beamie
An agency managing 20 SMB clients installs Beamix Agency Edition ($1,999/mo). They get:

- **20 Beamies** — one per client, each with its own memory, voice localization, and personality. Agency owner can toggle between clients, and each client's Beamie greets them with client-specific context: "Yael's site lost 2 citations this week." Next client: "Avi's FAQ agent improved their Perplexity rank from 8 to 3."
- **The Meta-Beamie** — a larger, slightly different character (silver + gold, "senior partner" personality) who watches all 20 clients from a portfolio view. Meta-Beamie ranks clients by priority ("3 clients need attention: Yael's Blooma problem is worsening, Avi's schema just broke, Dana's competitor launched a new product"), can brief the agency owner on the full portfolio in 30 seconds of voice, and runs weekly auto-generated "agency reports" per client with personalized commentary.
- **The Shared Inbox** — account-wide view of all pending approvals across all clients. Agency owner can bulk-approve safe suggestions, delegate approvals to account managers, and see team throughput.
- **White-Label Permalinks** — the shareable scan replay (Idea 3) is branded with the agency's logo when shared to clients. Beamix watermark becomes small.

What changes about v2: v2 is single-user B2B. v2-courage makes Beamix a dual-product: B2B SaaS for SMBs ($79-$499) and B2B2B platform for agencies ($1,999-$9,999). Meta-Beamie is the category-defining feature that makes Beamix the dominant AI-agency tool in 18 months.

### Idea 8 — "Beamie reads the room" — emotional UI tuned to context, not mood
Caveat up front: we do not read the user's emotions via camera or biometrics. That's the ethical line. What we *do* read is context — signals the user is already broadcasting by interacting with the product:

- **Typing speed / interaction pace:** If the user is typing fast and clicking quickly, Beamie's response latency drops (answers arrive faster, animations trim), and Beamie's text gets more terse ("Done. Let me know."). If the user is slow and deliberating, Beamie waits longer, uses fuller sentences, and offers more detail.
- **Time of day + day of week:** Beamie at 22:00 on a Thursday doesn't propose a 3-hour content audit — it offers "something light for tonight." Beamie Monday morning opens with yesterday's weekend digest.
- **Tab-away frequency:** If the user is switching tabs every 8 seconds, Beamie delays its nudges. The user is in research mode; this is not the moment to interrupt.
- **Frustration signals:** If the user types a message into Beamie's text field and then deletes it 3 times in a row, Beamie's response softens: "Take your time. I'm here when you're ready. No rush." (Learned from observation, not assumed.)
- **The "after a loss" mode:** If a scan shows a score drop of 10+ points, Beamie's tint is slightly less confident for the session, and the first sentence Beamie says is empathic ("I know, this one stings"), not performative ("Don't worry!"). This is the hardest thing to get right. Calibrated by a dedicated emotion-copy editor.

The ethical rail: every emotion-adaptive behavior is a user-facing setting, defaulting to on, toggleable off in Settings > Beamie > Adaptive Response. Transparency: a memory chip says "Adapts to your pace and time of day." The system is never *predictive* of mood — only *responsive* to observed interaction signals the user already broadcasts.

What changes about v2: v2 is context-agnostic. v2-courage treats every interaction as context-aware in small, honest ways. The product feels *attuned*, not just functional.

### Idea 9 — "Beamie proposes, not just executes" — the colleague who notices things before you ask
Every morning at 07:00 local time (user-configured), Beamie runs a silent internal "scan of the world" using the prior 24 hours of scan data across all of Beamix's user base plus public AI-search changes it can detect (Perplexity trend shifts, ChatGPT behavior changes, Gemini rollouts). Beamie then curates, *for you specifically*, a Morning Note — a single card in the Inbox titled "Beamie noticed…" with up to 3 observations:

- "Your competitor Blooma got cited 3 times this week on Perplexity — up from 0. Want me to investigate their content strategy?" [Run Competitor Intelligence →]
- "ChatGPT started answering 'best [your industry] in [your city]' questions this week. You're not mentioned. Want me to propose a fix?" [Run Content Optimizer →]
- "Google AI Overviews rolled out a new citation format yesterday. 7 of your pages are affected. Want me to check?" [Run Freshness Monitor →]

Each observation is a proposal with a one-click approve. Beamie is a colleague who walks into your office with three things you should know, not a tool waiting for you to click. This is the "proactive automation model" from v2 but 10x more ambitious — not just "here are agent suggestions based on your site" but "here are things happening *in the world* that affect you, curated by a partner."

What changes about v2: v2 proposes agent runs based on your site's gaps. v2-courage proposes based on *the world changing around you*. Beamie is not just observant about your product — Beamie is observant about your market.

### Idea 10 — "The Beamix Origin Story" — onboarding as narrative, not tutorial
No 3-step tour. No dots. No tooltips. The first time a user lands on Beamix, they go through **The Origin Story** — a 2-minute, skippable, cinematic experience:

- Frame 1: Beamie fades in. Says (text, optionally voice): "I'm Beamie. Nice to meet you. Before we start, can I ask your name?" User types "Yael." Beamie responds: "Hi, Yael."
- Frame 2: "Tell me about your business in one sentence, in whatever language feels right." User writes (Hebrew): "אני עצמאית שעוזרת לסטארטאפים לעצב את המוצר שלהם." Beamie processes. "Got it. You help startups design product. I'll call your business design consulting. Sound right?" Yael confirms.
- Frame 3: "What keeps you up at night about your business's visibility?" User writes. Beamie listens. Says "Okay. That tells me a lot."
- Frame 4: The first scan runs. Full Stage, 30 seconds. The user watches, for the first time, Beamie courier their site to the 7 AI engines. The score arrives. Beamie says: "Here's where you stand. 47 out of 100. That's fair. Let me show you what that means."
- Frame 5: Beamie narrates the top 3 findings, each with 1 sentence of context. "Here's the first one: ChatGPT thinks you do software development, not design. We can fix this. Want me to start?"
- Frame 6: Yael clicks yes. The first agent runs, in a mini-Stage. 60 seconds. Beamie narrates. The agent produces a suggested fix. Yael approves or not. Either way, Beamie says: "That's your first Beamix moment. From here, I'll keep watching. You don't have to open the app every day. I'll come to you when something matters."
- Frame 7: Beamie walks to its final resting position in the bottom-right corner. Fades to idle. The product is now "running."

The Origin Story replaces 90% of onboarding copy. It is a *relationship-forming moment*, not a feature tour. Users remember Beamie's first question ("what keeps you up at night?") for months. That answer becomes a permanent memory Beamie can reference: "Remember when you told me X was keeping you up at night?" Three months later, Beamie can show you how that specific fear was addressed.

What changes about v2: v2 has a 3-step pointing tour that happens inside the product. v2-courage replaces all onboarding with a narrative that establishes the Beamie-user relationship *first*, then uses that relationship to drive every subsequent interaction.

## 3. THE TRUE FIRST SCAN REVEAL (reimagined)

v2's First Scan Reveal is excellent: 30-second Stage, public URL, no signup gate, score appears before commitment ask. Good. Here is what it becomes if we remove all restraints.

### The Pre-Scan Ritual (0-3 seconds)
The user types their domain into the input field. As they type, Beamie peeks out from behind the input — a subtle state change: Beamie's eyes track the characters as they appear. When the user hits Enter, Beamie doesn't just start — Beamie pauses, does a tiny "readying" gesture (shoulders settle, magnifying glass materializes in hand), and says, in a single line of InterDisplay-italic text beneath the input: "Okay. Let me show you." This tiny 2-second pre-ritual sets the tone that what's about to happen is a performance, not a process.

### The Stage in 3D Depth (3-28 seconds)
The Stage is not flat. The canvas has *depth* — a Linear-Rauno-level z-layer choreography. The user's site card sits in the front plane. The 7 engine orbs sit in a middle plane, softly back-of-focus until Beamie visits them (then they sharpen and come forward). Beamie exists in a plane that moves freely. The background has a slow, imperceptible parallax as Beamie moves — giving the whole scene gentle life.

The engines themselves are not logos on pills. They are **orbs** — small 3D-ish spheres (SVG + CSS filters, or WebGL for fidelity) with their logo embossed at the center. Each orb has a unique ambient pulse: ChatGPT orb pulses at ChatGPT-green, Perplexity at Perplexity-teal, Claude at Anthropic-orange, Gemini at Google-blue, Grok at X-black, You.com at You-purple, Google AIO at Google-pastel. The orbs are *different in aesthetic* — the user feels the seven engines as distinct entities, not stamped copies.

When Beamie enters an orb, the orb momentarily *becomes the stage* — the background shifts to that engine's brand gradient for 2 seconds, labeled with a large softly-typed engine name. This is cinematic: the user *visits* ChatGPT, doesn't just send data to ChatGPT. Then back to the neutral stage. The rhythm of visiting 7 different "environments" in 30 seconds is hypnotic.

### The Narration in Voice and Text (parallel)
v2's Stage has a text side-panel. v2-courage has an optional voice narration toggle (default on for first scan, off for subsequent). Beamie's voice (Idea 5) narrates in real-time, in the user's chosen language: "Okay, I'm asking ChatGPT now… ChatGPT knows about you, but thinks you're a different kind of business. Let me ask Perplexity next…" The voice is warm, conversational, slightly unhurried. Each engine visit has a scripted-but-variable narration that references what Beamie found. This makes the scan feel like a nature documentary where you are the subject.

### The Climactic Score Reveal (28-32 seconds)
Standard score ring fill, but with one addition: when the number lands on the user's score, Beamie walks to the ring, places a hand on it, and says (voice + text): "Here's where you stand." Then a one-second pause. Then: "Let me explain."

### The Post-Scan Moment (32-60 seconds)
Immediately after the score reveals, Beamie walks the user through the top 3 findings one at a time. Not as a list of cards — as a *guided explanation*. Beamie points to the first finding, it animates forward, Beamie explains it in one conversational sentence, and a "Fix this?" button appears. User clicks yes → the fix agent starts in a mini-Stage within the current view. User clicks "Later" → Beamie moves to finding 2. This is a live, interactive post-scan segment — the user gets to *do work* before they've even signed up. They experience a completed agent run (maybe a FAQ fix) *before* they see a signup modal.

### The Shareable Moment (0-∞ seconds after)
At any point during or after the scan, a small "Share this" button appears (at the top-right of the Stage, subtle but visible). Clicking it:

1. Pauses the scan replay (if running)
2. Shows 4 share surfaces: Permalink, Auto-GIF, Report Card PNG, Twitter Thread (Idea 3)
3. Generates them server-side in under 2 seconds
4. Provides a single-click copy for each

The share button is not at the end — it's present throughout, because users emotionally decide to share mid-experience.

### The Signup Invitation (60-∞ seconds after)
No modal. No overlay. A single, persistent bottom bar: "Want Beamie to keep watching? Create a free account — your scan will save automatically." One button. Beamie stands near it, glances at it occasionally. Does not nag. If the user leaves and comes back within 7 days, the scan is still there — Beamie remembers which scan belongs to which browser via a cookie + fingerprint. On signup, the scan, the share history, and the user's "name" if given during the Origin Story migrate into the new account.

### The Viral Loop
Every shared scan replay has a "Scan yours" button at the end. This button, when clicked, starts a new scan with the viewer's domain — which loads the Stage experience from scratch. Each viewer becomes a potential user. Each user becomes a potential distributor. K-factor > 1 by design.

## 4. THE BEAMIE PERSONALITY ARC

Beamie is not a static character. Beamie evolves over the user's relationship with the product. Here is the 12-month arc.

### Day 1 — The New Colleague
Beamie is formal, warm, slightly tentative. Uses full names. Explains everything. Doesn't assume. Asks clarifying questions. Volume: high. Beamie speaks more on day 1 because there is no shared context yet. The Origin Story establishes the basic relational facts: your name, your business, your fear, your first win.

Visual state: Default silver, neutral. Face is open, slightly bright-eyed. Movement: slightly more deliberate, as if still learning the space.

### Day 30 — The Familiar Face
Beamie has learned your rhythm. Knows your business category. Knows your top 3 competitors. Has seen your work on 4-6 scans. Speaks less, because shared context has replaced explanation. Uses shortcut phrases: "Another Blooma moment" instead of "Your competitor Blooma was mentioned again."

Visual state: Body very subtly warmer-toned (silver → silver with 5% warm overtone). Eyes slightly less wide — relaxed attentiveness. Movement: more fluid, slightly faster.

### Day 90 — The Partner
Beamie has been through 12 scans, 30+ agent runs. Beamie now makes *proactive* suggestions the user didn't ask for (Idea 9). Beamie references specific prior moments: "Remember the schema fix from October that you were worried about? It's still working." Beamie's voice (literally, if voice is on) is a fraction more casual. Takes small liberties: "Honestly, I'd skip this one."

Visual state: Body has a personal patina — a very subtle accumulated color shift the user can see when compared side-by-side to a new user's Beamie. Face has learned micro-expressions specific to this user's interaction patterns.

### Day 180 — The Trusted Advisor
Beamie's memory is deep. Beamie can tell you "your best month was November, here's what we did that month." Beamie now has opinions: "I don't think this agent is a good fit for your brand voice — want me to explain why?" Beamie's Hebrew humor kicks in for users who have opted in. Beamie might say "יאללה" before a scan.

Visual state: Mature Beamie. Slightly more settled in its movements. Possibly (a user-toggleable cosmetic) a small badge or accessory that reflects a shared milestone — "the Tuesday breakthrough" might manifest as a tiny visual charm on Beamie that only this user's Beamie has. Status-by-relationship-duration.

### Month 12 — The Co-Conspirator
Beamie knows you. Remembers your first win by name and date. Knows the day of the week you're most productive. Knows which competitors make you anxious. Knows which agent outputs you almost always reject and which you almost always approve — and predicts ahead. Some agents are better when paired. FAQ + Schema. Content Optimizer + Freshness Monitor. When the user approves a paired agent run, the Stage becomes a **duet scene**: two agents visually occupy the canvas, each in their own lane, working in parallel. Their paths cross mid-canvas (data hand-off). The user sees, literally, the agents *collaborating*. Side panel shows both plan lists running in parallel, with cross-references when one agent passes data to the other. This makes agent-chaining (a technical concept) feel like a *theater performance* — two dancers on stage, choreographed. Zero competitor has this.

### A3 — "Beamie's Dreams" — the overnight insight surface
While the user sleeps (based on their timezone and typical sleep hours — learned over time), Beamix runs overnight analysis: deeper scans, competitor monitoring, trend detection. Every agent action is reversible — one-click undo up to 30 days post-run. But Beamix's undo is not just "revert." It's narrative: when a user undoes an agent's work, Beamie asks, conversationally (voice optional): "What didn't work?" Options: "The tone was off" / "The suggestion didn't fit my audience" / "I changed my mind" / "Write your own reason." Beamie commits this to memory. Future runs of the same agent avoid this pattern. Over time, every agent *learns this user's taste*. Undo becomes training data, and every user's Beamix becomes subtly custom-trained on their preferences. The agent that rejects 80% of schema suggestions becomes the agent that eventually only suggests schema the user will approve. Zero configuration. All learned from undo + context.

### A5 — "The Clock" — the on-stage ticking clock that marks the days since launch
Somewhere in the product shell — perhaps in the Home header, small, beside the score — is a running counter: "Day 127 with Beamie." Small, understated. Clickable. Opens a scroll-through timeline of key moments: first scan, first agent run, first citation, first win, every milestone. This is a *relationship UI* — a subtle reminder that the user has invested time into this product, and the product has invested time into them. Reduces churn dramatically (research-backed pattern from Duolingo streaks, Headspace day counts). For Beamix, it ties users emotionally to continuation.

### A6 — "The Co-Pilot Handoff" — Beamie can hand you to a human
When an agent hits a genuine complexity wall — a schema question too nuanced for the current agent, a brand-voice question Beamie doesn't have enough context for — Beamie says: "This one's above my pay grade. Users don't feel abandoned by the AI — they feel escorted to the right resource. Revenue opportunity: premium "Human review" add-on ($49/mo on Build, free on Scale). Brand opportunity: Beamix is honest about what AI can and can't do — a rare stance in 2026-2027.

Scale tier feature: the user can input a competitor domain, and Beamix runs a parallel scan on them, shown as a **ghost shadow Stage** — identical to their own scan stage, but tinted gray, with a dotted-line Beamie doing the same courier work. The two scans play side-by-side. User can see, viscerally, where they outrank and where they lose. This is "Clash of Clans replay UX applied to AI search" — competitive, kinetic, unforgettable. Sharable as a side-by-side replay (Idea 3).

# APPENDIX B — THE SPATIAL STAGE IN DETAIL
