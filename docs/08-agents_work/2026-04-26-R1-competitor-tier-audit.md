## Competitive Gap Map — Synthesis

### What competitors gate behind top tiers (the upsell playbook):

| Upsell Lever | Who Does It | Gate Point |
|---|---|---|
| **AI engine count** | Profound (1→3→10+), Peec (3→6), Otterly (4 base + add-ons) | Most powerful upsell — forces upgrade to see full picture |
| **AI agents / content optimization** | Profound (Growth+), AthenaHQ (basic at $295, advanced at Enterprise) | Content action is the #1 Enterprise lock-in |
| **Citation Engine / prediction** | AthenaHQ ACE (Enterprise-only) | Unique — no other competitor offers citation probability modeling |
| **API access** | AthenaHQ, Profound, Peec (all Enterprise-only) | Standard for Enterprise lock-in |
| **Multi-region / multi-language** | AthenaHQ (Enterprise), Peec (Advanced+) | Significant for international SMBs |
| **BI integrations** (Tableau, Looker, Power BI) | AthenaHQ (Enterprise), Otterly (Standard+, Looker only), Peec (Advanced+) | Data teams demand this |
| **Agency / multi-client** | Peec (dedicated agency plans with pitch workspaces) | Only Peec has a true agency pricing model |
| **Prompt volume research** | Profound (Enterprise-only) | Unique dataset — AI search demand intelligence |
| **White-label** | None confirmed publicly | Gap across the market |

### The single most valuable upsell across competitors:

**AI engine gating + AI-powered content action.** Profound and AthenaHQ both lock their most valuable features (full engine coverage + AI agents that generate/optimize content) behind $400+/mo or Enterprise tiers. The message is clear: monitoring is cheap; *fixing visibility* is the premium.

### Where Beamix $499 Scale tier may underdeliver vs market:

1. **Citation prediction engine** — AthenaHQ's ACE predicts citation probability and prescribes content changes. No Beamix equivalent described. This is the most defensible Enterprise feature in the market.
2. **Prompt Volume data** — Profound's exclusive dataset showing what users actually ask AI engines (search demand for AI, not traditional SEO). Beamix has no equivalent intelligence layer.
3. **Agency/multi-client billing** — Peec offers dedicated agency plans with pitch workspaces, per-client project allocation, and centralized billing. Beamix Scale supports multi-domain but lacks agency-specific workflow tools (pitch demos, per-client reporting, reseller billing).
4. **BI tool integrations** — AthenaHQ offers Tableau, Power BI, and Looker at Enterprise. Beamix should consider at least Looker Studio at Scale.

### Where Beamix can leap ahead:

1. **Agents that DO the work** — Most competitors stop at "recommendations" or "content briefs." Beamix's 11 specialized GEO agents that execute changes (not just suggest them) is genuinely differentiated. Profound has basic content drafting; AthenaHQ has optimization suggestions. Neither deploys autonomous agents that fix issues end-to-end.
2. **Proactive automation model** — Beamix's "suggestions -> approve -> agents run -> review in Inbox" workflow has no equivalent in any competitor. Every competitor requires manual action on recommendations.
3. **Price-to-engine ratio** — Beamix covers 6+ AI engines from the $79 Discover tier. Profound charges $99 for 1 engine. Otterly charges $29 for 4. AthenaHQ charges $295 for 8. Beamix's value at entry is competitive.
4. **No white-label exists anywhere** — First mover on white-label GEO reporting at Scale tier would be a genuine market gap capture.
