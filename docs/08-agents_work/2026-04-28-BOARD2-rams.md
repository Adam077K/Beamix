# Beamix Design Review — Through Dieter Rams' Lens
Date: 2026-04-28
Reviewer: Senior Designer applying Rams' 10 principles + Vitsoe discipline

---

## A — What's right

A small number of decisions in this body of work pass the principles cleanly. Name them, then stop being generous.

**The token discipline (Design System §1).** Ten type sizes, locked. Eight spacing values, locked, with two named exceptions, each justified. One brand colour on chrome; semantic colour quarantined to data. Borders defined as opacity, not hex, so dark mode is a derivation, not a parallel system. This is 606-shelving logic applied to a CSS file: a small kit of parts that recombines into every page. Principle 4 (understandable), principle 10 (as little design as possible). The constraint is the system. Keep it.

**The single chrome-colour rule.** `--color-brand` (`#3370FF`) is permitted only on chrome; data colour never touches a button. This is the rule that separates Beamix from Notion-grade chaos. It is the single most Rams-grade sentence in the document. Honour it ruthlessly.

**The 56px topbar as the entire chrome.** No left sidebar at MVP. Nav is seven words across the top. The canvas is the page, not a peer of the page. Principle 5 (unobtrusive). Most product designers would not have the discipline to refuse a sidebar; this team did. Note this — it is rare.

**The TopbarStatus dot.** Six pixels. One colour. Two opacities. Communicates "system is acting" at the lowest possible visual cost. This is a Braun ET66 calculator-key kind of move: one component, doing one thing, completely. Principle 2 (useful), principle 10. The dot is the best component in the system.

**The decision to never repeat entrance animations within a session.** Buried in §3, but it is the most disciplined animation rule I have read in any product spec this year. Motion communicates state transitions, not presence. A pulsing product that is not acting is lying. The author understands Rams' principle 6 (honest) better than the rest of the document does.

**The /security page register.** Not because of cream paper — that is its problem — but because the page refuses tabs, refuses motion, refuses count-up, and treats the security disclosure as an artifact that is "already-arrived." That refusal is correct. Aria's review takes 6 minutes because the page is structured for it, not styled for it. Principle 4 again.

**The empty state restraint.** Anti-pattern §7 forbids illustrative empty states except /inbox. Most teams ship a Storyset illustration on every empty surface. Beamix forbade it. Approve.

The rest needs work.

## B — What violates the principles

I will move surface by surface and name the violations. The team has confused craft with proliferation. There is too much. Less, but better — not less and more.

**The 4-mark sigil system. Three earn their place. One does not.**

The Ring earns it. It is geometry, not decoration. The 252° arc with a 30° gap reads as a frame around the score, not as ornament; the gap-pulse during action is a state signal, not a flourish. The deterministic Rough.js terminus is a single hand-mark inside a CSS arc — the seam Adam wants. Principle 3 (aesthetic), 4 (understandable), 8 (thorough). Keep.

The Trace earns it conditionally. A 28%-opacity hand-drawn underline beneath text Beamix has touched in the last 24 hours is ambient evidence, not decoration — it carries information (recency). But the spec lets it appear in too many places: /home, /competitors, /inbox filter labels, /workspace step output, the /security left rail, the /onboarding signature, the workflow inspector. When a mark appears that often, it stops carrying information. **Reduce its surfaces by half.** The /security left rail Trace is the worst offender — a security disclosure does not need a hand-drawn underline. Delete it there.

The Seal earns it, narrowly. Authorship marks on artifacts (Monthly Update PDF, Brief, /scan public footer, OG card) are honest. But the Seal on Inbox approve-button hover (foreshadowing the seal as the user mouses over a button) is a Rams violation: **the seal is announcing itself** before authorship has occurred. Principle 6 (honest) — the seal means "Beamix has signed this." A seal that draws in anticipation is theatre. Delete the Inbox-hover Seal.

The Margin does not earn its place. A 24px-wide left strip on artifact surfaces filling with deterministic Rough.js circles, one per agent action, "the product's memory made visible" — this is decoration with a story attached. Principle 2: every element earns its place. What does the Margin actually communicate that the activity log does not? It produces visual texture on the side of /scans rows, /workspace steps, Monthly Update PDFs. It is a small, drawn, accumulating ornament. **The Margin is the system's vestigial organ. Cut it.**

**The 12 motion tokens. At least four are redundant.**

`motion/ring-pulse` and `motion/topbar-status` both signal "agent acting" with opacity loops at different cadences. One is enough; the topbar dot is the cheaper, smaller, more honest signal. Delete `ring-pulse` — let the Ring stay still. Acting is communicated by the topbar dot and the per-step indicators in /workspace.

`motion/score-fill` is a count-up animation. Rams would call this fashion. The score is a fact at page-load time; counting from zero is theatre that says "we are calculating," when in fact the calculation has already completed on the server. Principle 6 (honest). Delete.

`motion/microcopy-rotate` (substep cross-fade in /workspace, 200ms cross-fade + 800ms hold) is the kind of cleverness that announces the product is working. The /workspace surface already shows step state. The rotating microcopy is a personality move dressed as feedback. Delete or downgrade to a single static "Working…" line.

`motion/signature-stroke` after `motion/seal-draw`, choreographed to 600ms after the seal begins — the Brief signature ceremony. This is the one I would defend last. It is decoration with a narrative. See Section C.

**The cream-paper editorial register. Half of its uses are earned. Half are not.**

Earned: Monthly Update PDF, /scan public hero, OG share card, Monday Digest header strip, the Brief itself. These are artifacts the customer keeps, forwards, screenshots. The cream signals "this is to keep," and that is a function, not a style.

Unearned: the /security page on cream paper. /security is a technical disclosure, not a kept artifact. Aria does not photograph it, frame it, or forward it to his board. He scans it and closes the tab. Wrapping it in cream paper imports the Brief's emotional register into a document that should be clinical. Worse, the spec then nests "white-paper-island spec blocks" inside cream — a register-shift inside a register-shift. This is fashion. The /security page should be on `--color-paper` (white), Inter throughout, Fraunces banished. Vitsoe's furniture catalogue has no cream paper; it is white because the furniture is the content.

Unearned: the cream-paper Editorial cell embedded inside the Workflow Builder's Admin Utility inspector. Brief grounding inside a clinical surface is a strong concept — but staging it as Fraunces 300 italic on cream paper inside a 320px clinical sidebar is the design that announces itself loudest in the entire system. The customer does not need a register-shift to be told this is from the Brief; a 1px left rule + label "Authorized by your Brief" + the quoted text in Inter 400 italic would carry the same meaning at 30% of the visual cost. This is the moment the team confused "distinctive" with "good." See Section E for what to do instead.

**Hand-drawn (Rough.js) elements. The proliferation is the problem.**

Rough.js shows up on: the Ring terminus, the Trace, the Seal, the Margin, agent monograms (CrewMonogram), empty-state illustrations (laptop-leaf), the /security left-rail underlines, the workflow trigger node's cream wash, the /scan storyboard's pencil-stroke underline. Eight surfaces. The hand-drawn mark earns its place when it carries meaning the geometric mark cannot. The Trace (recency), the Seal (authorship), and the Ring terminus (the seam between machine and hand) each carry meaning. The CrewMonogram does not — it is a circle with a letter, drawn rough so the team can claim a signature. Vitsoe would render it as a circle. So would I.

**The Activity Ring's claim to be "the single image of Beamix."** It is large enough (200px), it is custom enough (the seam), it lives in only the right places (/home above-fold, /scan public, Monthly Update header, OG card). The claim survives, narrowly. But the spec says the Ring "draws in from 0 to 252° over 1500ms as part of the onboarding boot sequence" — 1.5 seconds is fashion, not communication. Principle 7 (long-lasting): an image that survives 50 years does not perform on entrance.

**The "yearbook" framing in /crew profile pages.** The board retired yearbook-as-default and preserved it for "ceremonial states only" — the empty state and per-agent profile pages. This is the worst of both options. Either yearbook is the metaphor, or it is not. A vestigial yearbook surfacing on the empty state is a brand decision wearing the costume of restraint.

**The signature line ceremony.** "— Beamix" in 22px Fraunces 300 italic, drawn with `motion/signature-stroke` 600ms after `motion/seal-draw` completes. This is the most self-aware moment in the system. Principle 5 (unobtrusive): the product should leave room for the user, not perform its own signing. Adam said no AI labels on output. The signature ceremony is the inverse problem — Beamix is labelling its own output, ceremonially, every time. The mark is honest; the ceremony is theatre. Cut the motion. Keep the static signature line as an ink stamp.

**Four visual registers.** Editorial Artifact, Journey Canvas, Data Intelligence, Admin Utility. One register too many. The Journey Canvas (used on /scan, /onboarding) and the Editorial Artifact (Brief, Monthly Update) overlap by 80% — both cream paper, both Fraunces, both Rough.js, both signed. Collapse them into one. Three registers: Artifact (cream, signed), Working (white product chrome), Disclosure (white, clinical, dateline-stamped — /security, /legal, /pricing receipts). Each with one mood, one job. Principle 10.

## C — Specific cuts I would make

In order of priority, ruthless.

1. **Delete the Margin.** All 24px-wide accumulation strips on /scans rows, /workspace steps, Monthly Update PDF, Monday Digest, /home Receipts, /competitors rows. The activity log carries this information textually. The Margin is decoration with a story. Save: about 24px of horizontal real estate per row, plus six Rough.js surfaces.

2. **Delete `motion/ring-pulse`.** Let the Ring stand still. The TopbarStatus dot pulses; that is enough. Two pulsing elements at different cadences is fashion.

3. **Delete `motion/score-fill`.** The score is a fact. Counting up performs calculation that has already completed. Principle 6.

4. **Delete `motion/signature-stroke` and `motion/seal-draw`.** Keep the static seal. Keep the static signature line. Lose the 800ms-plus-50ms-settle drama. The seal as a stamped ink mark is honest; as an animated signature, it is theatre.

5. **Delete `motion/microcopy-rotate` in /workspace.** Replace with a single static line per step: "Working." or the step's verb-noun summary. Cross-fading microcopy strings is personality, not feedback.

6. **Move /security off cream paper.** White surface, Inter throughout, Fraunces banished, no Trace under headings, no closing seal. The dateline ("LAST REVIEWED APR 27 2026" in Geist Mono) is the only signature the page needs. Aria reads facts faster on white.

7. **Delete the cream-paper Editorial cell inside the Workflow Builder inspector.** Replace with a 1px brand-blue left rule + label "Authorized by your Brief" in `text-xs` caps + the quoted clause in Inter 400 italic 14px + "— clause 2 of 4 · Edit Brief →" on a single line. The principle holds (the Brief grounds every node); the register-mixing falls away.

8. **Reduce the Trace to four surfaces.** Keep it on /home recent-activity rows, on /competitors editorial summary, on /workspace step output, on the Monday Digest header. Delete it from /security, /inbox filter labels, and the Workflow Builder inspector. Four uses, total. The mark stays meaningful.

9. **Delete the Inbox-approve-button-hover Seal-foreshadowing.** Authorship is signed on the artifact, not on the button.

10. **Collapse Journey Canvas register into Editorial Artifact register.** Three registers, total: Artifact, Working, Disclosure. Delete every spec sentence that distinguishes the four.

11. **Delete the yearbook ceremonial state on /crew empty page.** /crew empty is "0 agents installed." Show the roster in its empty rows; let the locked agents row carry the meaning.

12. **Delete the laptop-leaf Rough.js illustration on /inbox empty.** Replace with a single Fraunces line: *"Nothing needs you. Beamix is on it."* Centred, no illustration.

13. **Delete the "Cmd+K hint chip" floating below the workspace switcher for the first 7 days.** Tutorial chrome that vanishes is still tutorial chrome. Train via Cmd+K appearance in the search bar inside the open palette. One affordance per surface.

14. **Reduce font-feature usage.** `'cv11'` on every InterDisplay use, `'ss03'` on the score, `'ss01'` on Geist Mono and Fraunces, `'tnum'` everywhere — fine. But `'ss01'` on Fraunces is unnecessary cleverness; let the font stand. Minor cut. The principle: every feature flag must do work.

## D — What I would add

Almost nothing. Rams added rarely.

**One: a printed reference page.** Inside /settings, a single sub-page that, when printed at A4 portrait, produces a one-page card: the Truth File essentials, the active workflows, the active agents, the next 3 fire times. Geist Mono, ink, no decoration. Customers who run Beamix at scale (Yossi, Marcus) will print this. The 606 shelving system shipped with a paper catalogue because the system was meant to live in the customer's hand. Beamix should ship a printable ops card for the same reason. Total cost: one page, one print stylesheet.

**Two: a "what Beamix did not do" section on Monthly Update PDF.** The product publishes; Rams' principle 6 (honest) demands restraint also be reported. One line, near the closing seal: "Beamix considered 14 changes this month and rejected 6. Rejection log: [link]." This converts restraint into a visible product feature. No new register. No new motion. One line of Inter.

That is all.

## E — The single uncopyable craft move I champion

**The Brief grounding link, rendered as a 1px left rule + label + quoted clause + edit affordance, inside every agent action.**

Strip the cream paper. Strip the Fraunces. Strip the embedded register-shift inside the Admin Utility inspector. What remains is the actual move: every node, every recommendation, every published change, every Inbox row carries an inline citation back to the Brief clause that authorized it. The customer can click the citation and edit the source. The agent's authority is not "the system says so"; it is "you said so, in clause 2 of 4, on April 14th."

This is uncopyable not because it is a graphic treatment — graphic treatments are copied in a fortnight — but because it is a **structural commitment.** Every agent prompt is conditioned on the Brief. Every action stores `brief_clause_ref`. Every UI surface that shows an action queries that ref and renders the citation inline. The Brief is the constitution, and the product wears it on every page.

Vitsoe sells the 606 shelving system with a single-sheet diagram showing every shelf width, depth, and load capacity in one card. The product ships with its own grammar visible. Beamix's equivalent: every agent action visibly grounded in the Brief. Customers who scan one /workspace screen and see five Brief citations, each a click to edit, understand the system instantly. No marketing copy required. No video walkthrough required. The grammar is on the surface.

This is principle 4 (understandable), principle 6 (honest), principle 8 (thorough), and principle 10 (less, but better) all simultaneously. The visual treatment is small — a 1px rule and a label. The structural commitment is total. That is the difference.

Ship it without the cream paper. The cream paper is the part competitors will copy. The structural commitment is the part they will not.

## F — Direct response to other designer voices

**On the Senior Designer's "cream paper survives white-labeling" claim:** wrong frame. Cream paper does not survive white-labelling — it survives *being absent*. Scale-tier white-label digests sign as the agency's name; cream paper is one of the few brand-carrying choices Beamix has on those surfaces. That is not survival; that is *Beamix surviving the white-label*. Defensible only if cream paper carries function. Where it carries function (Brief, Monthly Update, /scan public, OG card), keep. Where it carries vibe (/security, the Workflow inspector cell, /onboarding Step 3), delete. The Senior Designer conflated "the cream paper is recognizable" with "the cream paper is necessary." Recognizable decoration is still decoration.

**On the Master Designer's "craft IS moat when coupled to product purpose" claim:** half-right. Coupled to product purpose, yes. Vitsoe's craft is in the load-bearing welds, not in the catalogue's typography. Beamix's craft moat lives in: (1) the Brief grounding citation on every agent action, (2) the Truth File as JSON-Schema-conformant export, (3) the audit log's append-only Postgres + S3 Object Lock, (4) the cross-engine scan calibration. None of those are Rough.js marks. The Master Designer pointed at decorative craft (the seal animation, the Margin, the signature ceremony) and claimed it was moat. It is not. It is what every well-funded competitor will copy in six months because copying graphic treatments is cheap. The structural commitments are what they cannot copy.

**Where I agree with both:** restraint as the dominant register, hand-drawn elements only when earned, cream as artifact-only, Fraunces as accent-only. They wrote those rules well. They then violated their own rules in the next document.

**Where I diverge:** the four-register taxonomy is one register too many. The 12-token motion vocabulary is four tokens too many. The Margin is not craft; it is decoration with a backstory. The signature-stroke ceremony is the system performing its own authority, which is the opposite of what Rams meant by honest. None of those criticisms damage the work that is right. They sharpen it.

A board does not need polite agreement. It needs a sharper version of the same principles, applied without favour.

## G — The Vitsoe-catalogue version of Beamix

Imagine the 1971 Vitsoe 606 catalogue. White paper. One photograph per spread. One line of caption. The shelf is shown empty, because the customer is meant to imagine their books on it. The product trusts the customer to bring the content.

The Vitsoe-catalogue Beamix:

**One register, three states.** White paper for the working surface. White paper for the disclosure surface. Cream paper for the artifact. Fraunces only on the artifact. Rough.js only on the Ring, the Trace, and the Seal. No Margin. No yearbook. No Journey Canvas. No register-shift cells. The system has fewer tools, used more thoroughly.

**One image: the Ring, still.** The Ring does not pulse. The Ring does not draw in. The Ring sits at the top of /home like a photograph in the catalogue — already-arrived, unchanging, evidence of the system's existence. State is communicated by the topbar dot and the per-row tokens. The Ring is the image, not the performance. Customers screenshot it because it is calm, not because it animates.

**One ceremony: the Brief.** The customer signs the Brief once. The product shows the Brief on every screen — quietly, as a 1px rule and a citation — never as a register-shift. The Brief is not a moment; it is a constitution. The ceremony is replaced by ubiquity.

**One typography hierarchy.** InterDisplay 500 for headings and the score. Inter 400 for everything else. Fraunces 300 only on the artifact and on the lede sentence of /home. Geist Mono only on technical specifics. Four families, four jobs, no overlaps.

**One paper card per customer per month.** The Monthly Update arrives as a permalink + PDF, formatted to A4, signed and sealed. Customers print it. Yossi prints one per client. Marcus's CFO files it. The product ships paper because paper is the format that survives. Vitsoe sold shelves; Beamix sells a monthly artifact that proves the shelves are full.

**One affordance per surface.** /home: approve. /inbox: approve. /scans: read. /security: read. /crew: configure. /workspace: watch. The product does not stack actions; it offers one. Every other affordance is one click deeper. Principle 5 (unobtrusive), principle 10 (as little design as possible).

This Beamix would not feature in a Stitch reel. It would feature in a museum, twenty years from now, beside the 606 shelving system, with the caption: *the product that did less, and meant it.*

---

## Closing — the one Rams principle Beamix is most violating today

Principle 10: *as little design as possible.* The team has shipped twelve motion tokens, four visual registers, four sigil marks, two Rough.js illustration patterns, and a register-shift inside a register-shift. Each decision is defensible in isolation; the aggregate is the violation. Less, but better — not less-is-the-floor-and-better-is-on-top.
