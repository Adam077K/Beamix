## §1 — The Beamie alternative (the strongest version)

### 1.1 What Beamie *is*, before we draw it

Beamie is not a mascot. Beamie is not a logo. Beamie is **the felt presence of the company doing the work**. The same way Pinch isn't a feature in Arc — Pinch is the fact that Arc *behaves like a thing*, not a tool. The Beamix question is: *when 18 specialized agents are doing the work, what does the customer feel they are talking to?* Frame 5 v2 v Locked answer: a brand. The Arc alternative: a small, named, drawn presence with eyes that follow the work.

The grammar is the same as Tamagotchi → Clippy (the failure mode) → Duolingo Owl (the redemption) → Finder face → Apple's Memoji → Linear's `L` glyph that sometimes blinks → Notion's faces in the empty states. Beamie sits where Linear's `L` glyph sits — in the corner — but with eight expressions and a small voice.

### 1.2 Beamie's design (described, not drawn)

**Shape language.** A single closed scribble built from three Bézier curves, drawn with the *same* Rough.js `seed` discipline Kare locked for the seal — but **not** an agent monogram. Beamie is the operator who introduces the monograms; Beamie sits at a higher level than any single agent. Geometry: a soft rounded-square head that's slightly heavier at the bottom (gives it a "settled, patient" weight, not a "bouncing app icon" weight). 1.5px stroke at 24px–48px, scales to 2px at 64–96px. Total stroke length budget: short enough that the entire mark feels readable as a single gesture, not a complex illustration. **No body.** Just face geometry. Body is implied by the speech bubble and by the page Beamie is standing on.

**Eye geometry.** Two open dots, 8% of head height, set on the upper-third horizontal line. Eye geometry never moves — only their *fill* and the curve under them changes, which makes facial states feel like states of *the same character*, not eight different characters. A 1px curve below the eyes is the entire mouth and eyebrow system combined — its geometry produces all 8 emotional states.

**Color.** Body stroke in `ink-1` (Beamix's ink, never brand-blue — Beamie is the operator, the brand-blue is reserved for the work). Eyes in `ink-1`. The mouth/expression curve picks up brand-blue *only* during `working` and `confirming` states (the moments when Beamie is acting on Beamix's behalf). This makes the brand color a *behavior* of Beamie, not a *property* of Beamie. Same logic Arc uses for Boost — color belongs to the action, not the actor.

**Asset weight.** Single SVG, ~5KB, no animation library required. The 8 facial states are 8 path strings the SVG component swaps between with a 200ms ease. The state transitions IS the animation. No Lottie, no Rive — this matters because every weight gain breaks the "Beamie is fast" feeling. Pinch's success in Arc is partly that it loads in zero perceived time; Beamie has to clear the same bar.

**Size table.**
- 16px favicon and topbar — Beamie collapses to a *single dot inside the seal*. The seal becomes Beamie's pupil. (This is the move that earns the favicon ownership Kare's #20 already gives the seal — but now the favicon is a *face* at favicon scale, which is uncopyable.)
- 24px corner avatar (mobile, dense surfaces) — Beamie's head + 1-line speech only.
- 48px standard (default desktop corner presence) — Beamie + speech bubble.
- 96px ceremonial (onboarding step 3, Cycle-Close Bell, Monthly Update PDF cover, Year-in-Review) — full editorial Beamie, hand-set Fraunces 300 italic in the bubble.

### 1.3 Beamie's 8 emotional states

Not "moods." States that map 1:1 to product states the customer already cares about. Each state is one path swap.

1. **Resting.** Eyes ink-1 dots, mouth curve neutral (slight downward arc — "patient, alert"). This is the default 95% of the time. *Triggered when:* no scan running, no agent acting, no inbox item pending.
2. **Scanning.** Eyes drift left-right on a 4-second loop (translate the dot positions ±1.5px on x-axis), mouth slight up-curve. *Triggered when:* a /scan public is running OR a customer's daily refresh is in flight. Beamie's eyes literally look at the work.
3. **Working.** Eyes ink-1, mouth becomes a 1px brand-blue dash (color migrates to the action, per §1.2). *Triggered when:* an agent is in mid-execution and has produced ≥1 provenance step.
4. **Finding.** Eyes briefly widen (radius +1px for 240ms), mouth tilts up at one corner only — small asymmetric smile. *Triggered when:* a citation result returns positive (customer cited at rank 1–3 on a new query). One-shot, never sustained.
5. **Surprised.** Eyes widen + mouth opens to a small `o` (closed circle, 4px). *Triggered when:* an unexpected event — first attributed call, first AI Overview citation, or a competitor disappears from a lead query. ≤1 per week budget. Scarcity is what makes it land.
6. **Concerned.** Eyebrow curve appears above the right eye (3px arc, ink-3), mouth goes flat. *Triggered when:* validator went into safe mode, Truth File integrity job flagged drift, or a Sev-1 alert. Beamie does not panic; Beamie is concerned. The face matches the operator-grade language Aria reads.
7. **Confirming.** Mouth becomes the seal mark itself, briefly (the seal "lives in Beamie's face" for 540ms). *Triggered when:* customer signs the Brief OR approves an inbox item. The seal's stamping ceremony is replaced by Beamie performing it from inside the face — *the seal is something Beamie does, not a graphic that appears.* This is the cleanest unification of two Frame 5 v2 marks (Beamie + Seal) in the alt vision.
8. **Completing.** Eyes close briefly (lines, not dots) — Beamie closes its eyes when the cycle closes, like Apple Watch's haptic acknowledgment. *Triggered when:* Cycle-Close Bell fires (F23). Frame 5 v2 has the Bell as a Ring gap closing; the Arc alt has Beamie blinking the cycle shut. Either works; only one is a felt presence.

### 1.4 Beamie's voice

Speech bubbles render in Fraunces 300 italic (the same register that Frame 5 v2 reserves for cream-paper artifacts) — but at 14px, never larger. **One sentence max.** Punctuation discipline: never an exclamation point. Beamie never gets excited; the customer gets excited. Beamie observes.

Sample utterances by surface (the *substitution* against Frame 5 v2's "Beamix" voice):

| Frame 5 v2 single-character voice | Arc alternative (Beamie) |
|---|---|
| "Beamix is working." | "I'm reading /pricing for FAQPage schema." (specific, verb-tense direct, "I" not "we") |
| "Beamix shipped your Schema fix." | "Schema fix shipped. Two engines re-crawled overnight." |
| "Inbox zero. Beamix is working." | "Inbox is empty. I'll come find you when something needs you." |
| "Cycle closed. {N} changes shipped this week." | "That's the cycle. Six changes this week. See you Monday." |
| "Beamix considered 14 changes this month and rejected 6." | "I considered fourteen. Six weren't right. Here's why →" |

The substitution test is uncomfortable. The Arc voice is *more memorable* and *less operator-grade*. Aria reads "I considered fourteen" and the cryptographic-trust posture quietly tilts. This is the cost we'll itemize in §6.

### 1.5 Surface placement (the felt-presence map)

- **`/home`** — upper-right, 48px, fixed-position avatar. Resting by default. Watches the Activity Ring; eyes drift toward it during scans. Cycle-Close Bell becomes Beamie blinking. Customer screenshots this.
- **`/onboarding`** — Beamie occupies the 96px ceremonial slot Adam already reserves for the Seal moment. Beamie walks the customer through the Brief in 8 micro-conversations (§4). Step 3 Seal becomes Beamie performing the seal (state 7 — confirming) in front of the customer.
- **`/inbox`** — 48px corner avatar **next to the Approve button**, not as page chrome. The bubble whispers the *one* reason this item routed here — `"This claim isn't in your Truth File yet. Want to add it?"` Beamie's reasoning replaces the cold validator-failure-reason copy from F4.
- **`/workspace`** — Beamie *is* the courier-flow figure Tufte killed. Tufte killed the walking figure because it was decorative; Beamie isn't decorative because Beamie *narrates the same execution column Ive proposed.* Tufte's narration column + Adam's walking figure unify into one move: Beamie sits at the top of the column, faces the active step, and the column is what Beamie is reading aloud.
- **`/scans` cartogram (F22)** — Beamie hovers at the corner of the 50×11 grid; on cell hover, Beamie's eyes look at the cell. *Beamie is the legend.* The cartogram becomes a thing being read by the operator, not just a thing rendered for the operator.
- **Workflow Builder** — during dry-run, Beamie walks each node in sequence, eyes on the active node, speech bubble narrating *"Schema Doctor would publish this at 09:00 Tuesday."* The builder gains the felt-quality that no DAG editor ever has. Retool, n8n, Make all explicitly avoided this. Yossi screenshots this and tweets it.
- **Mobile** — 32px corner avatar. Speech compresses to one phrase ("Inbox is clear."). Below 360px viewport: Beamie collapses to favicon scale, head only.

### 1.6 The Slack-style notification rewrite

Frame 5 v2: `Beamix shipped your Schema fix.`
Arc alternative: `Beamie just shipped your Schema fix.`

This is the smallest visible-surface move and the most consequential. It changes Beamix's identity from "the company name on the contract" to "a thing that does work for me." Stripe could never write this. Linear couldn't write this. *The cost is procurement-language drift* — section §6 holds this honest.

## §2 — Five branded behaviors not in current spec

Arc's discipline: branded behaviors > branded objects. Frame 5 v2 has objects (Ring, Seal, Trace, Margin, Monogram). It has motion tokens (12). It does **not** have branded *behaviors* — moments that the product *does to you* on a budget, repeatedly, that nobody else's product does. Five proposed:

### 2.1 The Tuck

**Trigger.** Customer's preferred quiet hours arrive (configured in /settings, default: 19:00–07:00 customer-local).
**Motion.** The Activity Ring slowly rotates 8° clockwise over 3.2s and *tucks* into a small notch on the right edge of its container, the way a watch hand parks at midnight. The Ring is now visibly "off-duty." A 12px Fraunces 300 italic line appears under the score: *"Sleeping until 07:00."*
**Frequency budget.** Once per day, observable only if customer is in the app at quiet-hours boundary.
**The felt thing.** Beamix has a body that rests. Customers tell their colleagues *"the dashboard literally goes to sleep"* — once. This is the line that ends up in a Twitter thread.

### 2.2 The Lift

**Trigger.** A citation row in /scans moves from rank ≥4 to rank 1–3 between two scans (i.e., Beamix's work caused a real ranking gain).
**Motion.** That row briefly translates 4px upward + a 200ms hold + a brand-blue 6px outer halo + settles back. The neighboring rows do not move; just the lifted row.
**Frequency budget.** Up to 5 per scan; if more rows would qualify, only the top 5 by delta lift.
**The felt thing.** Wins are physical. The data already exists; the moment is given a body. Nothing in Profound or Otterly does this — they show numbers going up, not the *act* of going up.

### 2.3 The Wave

**Trigger.** First /home load each Monday morning (in customer's local timezone), once per week.
**Motion.** The 11-engine small-multiples sparkline strip waves left-to-right with 60ms stagger before settling — but **only on Monday**. Tuesday–Sunday, it renders static at t=0 (matching Frame 5 v2 lock #2: cut path-draw on returning sessions). Monday is the exception.
**Frequency budget.** Once per week, exactly.
**The felt thing.** The product greets you. The week begins. *Calendar-ritual design.* This is the Apple-Watch-Sunday-summary equivalent for GEO. The motion violates Frame 5 v2's lock — that is intentional; we're proposing the lock have a Monday-shaped hole.

### 2.4 The Catch

**Trigger.** A competitor that was previously cited for a query Beamix has been working on *disappears from that query*. (i.e., your competitor lost ground to you.)
**Motion.** On /competitors row hover, the competitor row briefly shows a 1px ink-3 strikethrough animation across their name (240ms draw, 800ms hold, 240ms fade). Plus a Beamie state-4 (finding) reaction in the corner avatar: small asymmetric smile.
**Frequency budget.** Maximum 1 per scan; if multiple competitors fell, only the most-cited one earns the strikethrough.
**The felt thing.** Competitive *witness*. The product noticed your competitor lost ground. No competitor's product makes you feel watched-over by an ally. This is the move that drives renewal more than any KPI.

### 2.5 The Quiet

**Trigger.** A scan has been running for >12s and Beamix is in a thinking-heavy phase (multi-engine query orchestration). The customer has not interacted in 4s.
**Motion.** Every UI motion in the page slows by 30% (animation-duration multiplier). Hover transitions, button states, even cursor blink in inputs — everything becomes 30% slower for the duration of the heavy phase, returning to normal when the scan completes.
**Frequency budget.** Bounded by scan-execution windows; typically 1–2 times per day per active customer.
**The felt thing.** *The product breathes with the work it's doing.* Nobody notices this consciously the first time. By week 4, customers report "Beamix feels calmer than my other tools" without knowing why. Linear does something close to this with its loading states. Arc does this with the thumbnail-render slow-down during loading. The pattern is "computational state becomes felt state." Uncopyable because the engineering cost is real and the visible payoff is invisible — it only registers as *texture*.

## §3 — The "something to notice" cadence

Arc ships one delight per release. Frame 5 v2 has a /changelog (Linear-grade) but no canon for *what shows up there*. Frame 5 v2's monthly cadence is dominated by the Monthly Update PDF — that's a *customer-facing receipt*, not a *change-of-product moment*. Arc's discipline: a thing that wasn't there last month is there this month, and it's small, and it's loved.

**The proposal.** Every month, one branded behavior or one character moment ships. It appears in /changelog with editorial weight (Fraunces 300 italic headline + 2-line plain-text explanation + before/after artifact). It appears in the Monday Digest as the closing line: *"This month, Beamix learned to ___."*

The first six months proposed (six "somethings to notice"):

**Month 1 — The Ink Promotion.** The Seal stamps in pure `ink-1` instead of `brand-blue` on the Brief. Editorial register: black-on-cream, the way Apple's "Designed by Apple in California" book uses ink. The seal *grows up*. Changelog headline: *"The Seal stamps in ink now."*

**Month 2 — The Cycle-Close Bell speaks.** Until now the Bell rings the same closing sentence. Now it rings a sentence shaped by the customer's industry: *"That's the cycle. Six changes this week. Your slowest engine is Perplexity — three more queries on it next week."* The closing sentence becomes a private read of the data. Changelog headline: *"The Bell starts to know you."*

**Month 3 — Beamie's first private reaction.** A small, undocumented surprise: the *first* time a customer hits 90+ AI visibility score, Beamie blinks twice in succession (state 8, immediately then again 800ms later — a "double take"). Single-shot per customer per lifetime. Changelog headline: *"Beamie noticed something."* (No further detail in the public changelog. The customer who triggers it knows.)

**Month 4 — The Margin breathes.** On Monthly Update PDF page 1, the Margin's hand-drawn fingerprints now have temporal opacity (per Ive's #1 fix): current week 100%, prior month 20%, archived 6%. The artifact gains *time as a visual property.* Changelog headline: *"Older work fades."*

**Month 5 — The Wave goes vertical.** A second instance of branded motion from §2.3 arrives: when a customer crosses an attribution milestone (first 10 attributed calls, first 100, first $10K AOV), the corresponding KPI card briefly performs a small upward push (8px translate-y + brand-blue glow, 200ms hold). Repeats on every doubling. Changelog headline: *"Milestones lift."*

**Month 6 — The Print-Once-As-Gift artifact arrives.** The Ive #28 move (already in F26): customer's office mailbox receives the printed Monthly Update with wax seal. The changelog has *one image* of the printed artifact, photographed under available light. No copy. Changelog headline: *"We printed something for you."* (The /changelog publishes this on the day the package ships, not the day it arrives — the surprise lands in the mailbox first.)

**The discipline.** Each of these is one-engineer-week of work or less. Each one is the kind of thing that would have been cut as "polish" in any other product roadmap. Frame 5 v2 has the *infrastructure* to do this (changelog discipline, design tokens, PDF infra). It does not have the *cadence canon*. This is the gap.

## §4 — The hand-holding onboarding alternative

Frame 5 v2 onboarding (F2): 4 steps, customer in the driver's seat, Beamix observes, Brief is signed, Seal stamps. Operator-grade. The customer does the work; Beamix bears witness.

The Arc alternative: 8 micro-conversations, ≤2 lines each. Beamie initiates each one. The customer answers in chips or short text. The Brief *emerges from the conversation* — the customer doesn't write the Brief; the customer *talks to Beamie* and the Brief is the receipt.

### 4.1 The 8 micro-conversations

Each rendered in the same cream-paper editorial register Frame 5 v2 already uses for /onboarding step 3, but the entire flow gets the cream register, not just step 3.

1. **Beamie:** *"Hi. I'm here to do AI search work for your business. What do you sell?"*
   **Customer:** [chip menu — 7 vertical options + free text]
2. **Beamie:** *"Got it. {Vertical} — I know that vertical. Who's a customer that's perfect for you?"*
   **Customer:** [free text, ≤80 chars; Beamie tolerates fragments]
3. **Beamie:** *"Three words your customers would use to describe you. Not what your team would say — what they say."*
   **Customer:** [3 chips; Beamie suggests 5 from a Truth-File-vertical-defaults pool]
4. **Beamie:** *"Anything I should never say about your business? Examples: claims you can't make legally, or words you don't use."*
   **Customer:** [chips: "no medical claims", "no superlatives", "no jargon" — or free text]
5. **Beamie:** *"Who do you compete with that you'd want me watching?"*
   **Customer:** [domain entry, up to 5; Beamie auto-suggests 3 from vertical KG]
6. **Beamie:** *"I'll tag clicks from AI engines so you can see what I'm earning you. Should I send setup to your developer, or walk you through it now?"*
   **Customer:** [two-button — "Email my dev" / "Walk me through it"]
7. **Beamie:** *"Last thing. When something exceeds your guardrails, should I always ask, or should I act on the small stuff and tell you Monday?"*
   **Customer:** [autonomy radio — same canonical autonomy levels from F4]
8. **Beamie:** *"Here's what I heard. Read it, and if it sounds like your business, sign."*
   **[The Brief, fully rendered in Fraunces, populated from answers 1–7. Customer reads, optionally edits chips inline, then taps the seal. Beamie performs state 7 (confirming) — Beamie's mouth becomes the seal, stamps the Brief, returns to Beamie's face.]*
   **Beamie:** *"Thanks. I'll take it from here."*

### 4.2 What's gained

- **Warmth.** The first 90 seconds of product usage feel like talking to someone who already understood the problem. Frame 5 v2's onboarding feels like setting up a tool.
- **Retention compounds.** Customers who *speak* during onboarding remember the product. Speaking, even via chip, recruits memory consolidation that "filling in a form" does not. (This is the same mechanism behind Duolingo's first-day retention curve.)
- **Brief authorship feels mutual.** In Frame 5 v2 the Brief is "Beamix-authored, customer-approved" — the constitutional weight comes from the *Beamix* signing it. In the alt, the Brief is *co-authored in conversation*, which gives the customer real ownership without making them write it. The Brief becomes a *transcript of an agreement*, not a *document handed over.*
- **The 8th conversation's seal is unforgettable.** The customer signs and Beamie says *"Thanks. I'll take it from here."* That sentence is the line they screenshot. Frame 5 v2's "— Beamix" is read; "Thanks. I'll take it from here." is *remembered.*

### 4.3 What's lost

- **Operational confidence.** Aria, the hidden CTO buyer, opens the new account and reads the onboarding flow. Aria sees a face. Aria recommends Marcus to find a different vendor, because *"a serious AI infrastructure company doesn't have a Pikachu."* This is the strongest single argument against the alt and we are not going to soften it.
- **B2B procurement signals.** Beamie violates the "operator-grade" register that ICP Marcus's pricing sensitivity *and* Yossi's white-label posture both depend on. Yossi cannot resell a service named Beamie to a Tel Aviv law firm; he can resell a service named Beamix. The single-character lock is partly load-bearing for the Scale tier's reseller use case.
- **Scope creep risk.** Once Beamie speaks during onboarding, every empty state in the product wants Beamie to speak. The discipline of "Beamie appears only in 4 surfaces" is hard to hold, and a Beamie-everywhere product is the failure mode that makes Clippy a slur and not an ancestor.
- **The single-character voice canon is a clean code rule.** "No agent names in emails/PDFs/permalinks" is enforceable as a lint rule (it already is — see voice-canon-Model-B memory). Beamie introduces a third voice (Beamie / Beamix / agents) and the rule space grows quadratically. *Voice canon Model B's clean separation breaks.*

## §5 — The /home Beamie-resident alternative

### 5.1 What it looks like

Same 8 sections as F5. Same Activity Ring (still still — Frame 5 v2's #15 cut survives). Same KPI cards. Same sparkline rendered at t=0. **One change:** upper-right corner, 48px Beamie avatar, fixed-position. The avatar is part of the page chrome — it does not move on scroll, it does not animate on enter, it is just *there.* Always.

The avatar reacts to the same data the page already shows:
- **Resting** — default. Eyes neutral, mouth flat.
- **Scanning** — eyes drift toward the Activity Ring during refresh. (Reuses the existing TopbarStatus dot's "agent acting" signal, but as a face.)
- **Working** — mouth becomes brand-blue dash when an agent is mid-execution.
- **Finding** — small smile when a citation row in the Receipts list updates positively.
- **Concerned** — eyebrow appears when validator is in safe mode or review-debt counter is non-zero >7 days.
- **Completing** — eyes close on Cycle-Close Bell fire (replaces the Ring's gap-close).

Speech bubble appears on hover only — Beamie does not speak unless asked. Bubble contains the same "What's coming up" footer copy F5 already specifies, but framed as Beamie's read-back: *"Next scan Thursday. Next digest Monday. Next bill in 12 days."*

### 5.2 The trade

| Frame 5 v2 /home | Arc alt /home |
|---|---|
| Editorial dashboard. Calm. Operator-grade. Aria reads it without flinching. Marcus screenshots the Activity Ring. | Editorial dashboard with a small face in the corner. Calm. The face reacts to the data. Marcus screenshots the face. Aria has a one-eyebrow moment — survivable, but real. |
| The Activity Ring is "the single image of Beamix" (Frame 5 v2 §Designer). | Beamie is the single image of Beamix; the Ring is a chart inside Beamie's space. **Hierarchy inverts.** |
| The product respects the customer's seriousness. | The product is *companionable*. |
| Replicable with discipline + ten weeks. | Uncopyable for anyone who can't write a face. |

The trade is real. *Both versions are excellent products.* They are different companies.

## §6 — The verdict

### 6.1 The strongest defense of single-character (Frame 5 v2 is right)

1. **The hidden buyer is Aria, not Marcus.** PRD v3 §1 makes this explicit: Marcus's renewal in month 3 dies if Aria laughs at the security architecture. Aria is a CTO. Aria has spent 15 years filtering vendors by *"do they have a face on their dashboard."* Faces are the failure mode that disqualifies vendors *before* Aria reads the cryptographic posture. Beamie costs the company Aria, and Aria costs the company Marcus, and Marcus is half of the wedge. **The single-character lock is a load-bearing wall for the wedge ICP.** This is not a preference; it is a hire/no-hire signal in the procurement category Beamix sits in.
2. **Voice canon Model B is a clean rule that compounds across surfaces.** Two voices (agents-in-product / Beamix-on-artifacts) is enforceable, lintable, and doesn't drift across 31 features and 18 agents and 12 verticals. Three voices (Beamie / Beamix / agents) is a rule that *will be broken* in production by month 6, and the broken rule is more expensive than no rule. Frame 5 v2's voice discipline is the kind of cleanliness that survives 100 contributors; Arc's alt is the kind that needs Adam personally enforcing it forever.
3. **Yossi's Scale tier is a reseller. Beamie cannot be white-labeled.** The Scale-tier white-label config is per-client (per memory `project_white_label_per_client.md`). When Yossi's Tel Aviv law firm client receives a Monthly Update under Yossi's agency name, *Beamie cannot appear in it* — Beamie is Beamix, and Yossi's client never bought Beamix. So either Beamie is excluded from white-label surfaces (and the felt-presence promise breaks for Yossi's customers — who are Beamix's most strategic accounts) or Beamie is the central character (and white-label is impossible). **Frame 5 v2 single-character is the only model that survives the Scale tier's commercial reality.**

### 6.2 The strongest defense of character-led (Frame 5 v2 should reframe)

1. **The category is being created right now and is naming-up its operators.** Profound has no character. Otterly has no character. Athena AI has no character. *Beamix has 30 days to be the product whose operator has a name and a face before someone else ships theirs.* In a category where every competitor is a dashboard, the felt-presence move is the differentiator no spec sheet can match. Once one competitor ships their Pinch / Sky / Beamie, the move is unavailable forever.
2. **The brand artifacts (Monthly Update PDF, /scan public, Year-in-Review) are forwarded — they need a *thing* people remember between forwards.** When Marcus forwards the Monthly Update PDF to his board chair, the board chair reads it once and either *remembers* it next month or doesn't. The Frame 5 v2 PDF says "Beamix" in Fraunces. The Arc alt PDF says "Beamie" with a face on the cover. The face survives between forwards in a way the wordmark does not. The 18-month customer journey Worldbuilder spec'd assumes word-of-mouth as the renewal multiplier — *Beamie is the word that gets-of-mouthed.* Beamix is a product name; Beamie is a story.
3. **The product *already* has 18 named agents with monograms and personalities. Frame 5 v2's voice canon hides them externally. The Arc alt elevates *one* of them — Beamie, who is not an agent but the operator — and makes the existing agent personalities make sense.** Today, /crew shows 18 named agents that the rest of the product pretends don't exist. That asymmetry costs trust, not earns it. Beamie as an external face *legitimizes* the internal agent roster — *of course there are 18 specialists; Beamie is the manager who introduces them.* Frame 5 v2's structure is "Beamix the brand → no character externally → 18 characters internally hidden behind it." That structure has a missing layer. Beamie *is* the missing layer.

### 6.3 My call (Arc, honest)

**Single-character is right for Beamix's wedge.** Not because Beamie is wrong as a character — Beamie is excellent. But because Beamix's procurement reality (Aria), commercial reality (Yossi's white-label), and discipline reality (voice canon Model B as enforceable rule) all converge on the same answer: a face costs the company more than it earns *in this wedge*.

The alt version is a different company — call it *Beamie*, the consumer-feeling character-led GEO operator for very small SMBs and creators (Sarah's segment, deferred from the wedge). That company exists; it could be ours, post-wedge. It is not the company we are shipping in this PRD.

**However.** The single-character lock is right *with two narrow exceptions* that Adam should re-confirm explicitly:

1. **The Brief signing in onboarding deserves a felt presence.** The Seal alone is correct as a graphic mark; but the *moment* of signing is the trust-establishing peak of the entire customer journey, and the alt version's "Beamie performs the seal in front of the customer" is genuinely better than the alt-less version. Without going full character-led: consider letting the seal-stamp animation include a single small motion that *gestures toward an actor* — an ink-1 dot that briefly sits beside the seal during the 540ms stamp, then lifts away. *The hand that stamped it.* It costs nothing. It is the "Beamie's pupil" idea from §1.2 with the body removed. The seal earns a witness without earning a face.
2. **The Cycle-Close Bell is too small a moment for what it commemorates.** The cycle is the heart of the weekly product loop. ### 6.4 The middle path — does the 50-50 split work?

**Proposed split:** Beamie ships on /crew + onboarding + Monthly Update PDF cover. Single-character "Beamix" everywhere else (admin, /home, /inbox, /scans, /workspace, emails, permalinks, OG cards, marketing).
