# Board 3 — Seat 4: The Worldbuilder
## Designing the Beamix World, End-to-End

**Date:** 2026-04-26
**Seat:** Worldbuilder / Brand Architect
**Mandate:** Construct the entire ecosystem around Beamix as one coherent experience. Apple-tier integration of every customer touchpoint. The product is one surface; the world is the whole.

---

## Opening — what worldbuilding means here

Most SaaS companies build a product, slap a marketing site on it, hire a CSM, and call it an ecosystem. That's not what Apple did. That's not what Stripe did. That's not what Linear, Notion, Browser Company, or Anthropic did.

What those companies did is harder and more boring: they treated *every* surface — the product, the email, the docs, the changelog, the support page, the cancel-flow, the customer's own LinkedIn post — as a single artifact. The same voice. The same craft bar. The same point of view about the world. The same respect for the customer.

Beamix's CEO has stated the bar explicitly: billion-dollar feel, category-leading. Stripe / Linear / Apple / Anthropic-grade. Not "good." Category-defining. Every space, button, letter intentional.

The product (Frame 5) is now genuinely strong. What's missing — what is *always* missing in pre-launch SaaS — is the world the product lives in. This document specifies that world.

The world has six dimensions:
1. **Voice and worldview** — the soul that shows up in every word
2. **The customer journey** — 18 months from "never heard of you" to "won't switch"
3. **Asymmetric channels** — where $1 of effort earns $10 of trust
4. **The communications calendar** — every email, every cadence, every audience
5. **Community and customer success** — the human-feeling layer in an AI-agent product
6. **Brand artifacts** — the physical and digital objects that *are* the brand

Then three questions for the other three seats.

## Section 1 — The brand voice and worldview

### The voice (specific, quotable, codified)

Beamix's voice is built from four ingredients, in this order:

**1. Direct (חד לעניין). No fluff. No filler.**
Israeli sensibility, applied universally. No "We're excited to announce." No "We're on a mission to revolutionize." Beamix says the thing and gets out.

**2. Confident, not boastful.**
The product knows it works. It doesn't oversell. Because it knows, it doesn't need adjectives. Stripe-confident, not Salesforce-confident.

**3. Warm but operator-grade.**
The reader is a small-business owner with eleven other things to do. Beamix respects their time, doesn't waste their attention, doesn't pretend to be a friend, but isn't cold either. The voice of a senior engineer who genuinely likes you.

**4. Specific, with numbers.**
Anywhere there's a number, name it. Anywhere there's a mechanism, name it. *"This week 23 calls came in through Beamix."* not *"Customers are noticing the impact."*

Three quotable sentences that define the voice forever, used as North Star calibrations for every copywriter and AI agent that produces content under the brand:

> *"When customers ask ChatGPT for a plumber in your area, does your name come up? Probably not. Beamix fixes that."* — homepage hero

> *"This month: 47 phone calls and 12 form submissions came in through Beamix-attributed channels. That's up from 23 last month. The list is below."* — Monthly Update opener

> *"You've got 4 changes waiting for you. Most of them take 6 seconds to read. The rest of your inbox can wait."* — Monday Digest opener

Things the voice **never** does:
- Never says "We" without specifying who. ("We at Beamix" is fine; "We believe in the future of AI" is forbidden.)
- Never uses "synergy," "leverage" (as a verb), "unlock," "elevate," "supercharge," "empower," or any other SaaS-default verb.
- Never uses three exclamation points. Rarely uses one.
- Never apologizes preemptively ("Sorry to bother you, but...").
- Never explains AI search to a customer who clearly already understands it.
- Never says "AI-powered" anything. The customer doesn't care how it works.
- Never breaks the fourth wall to discuss its own AI nature ("As an AI agent, I...").

Things the voice **always** does:
- Always names the agent's action specifically. Not "we updated some things" — "we added 11 FAQ entries to your /pricing page."
- Always offers the rollback option when describing a change.
- Always uses Sarah's actual business name and her actual competitor names. The voice is personalized at the data layer, not just the salutation.
- Always closes with the next thing. Email, digest, push notification — every artifact ends by pointing to the next moment.
- Always signs off as "Beamix" externally. Never "your AI Visibility Crew." Never "Schema Doctor." (The roster is internal architecture; the voice is one.)

### The worldview

Beamix stands for one specific position about the world, and against another:

**For:** *AI search is the new front door of every business. Most small businesses are invisible to it. They shouldn't have to hire a $5,000/month consultant to fix that. The work itself should be done by software, the same way Stripe replaced "hire a payments lawyer."*

**Against:** *The dashboard industrial complex. Tools that show you you're losing without doing anything about it. The $99/mo vanity-metrics graveyard.*

**For:** *Ownership. The customer's FAQs, the customer's schema, the customer's site, the customer's data. Beamix is a contractor; the building belongs to the owner.*

**Against:** *The "we'll lock you in with proprietary content" strategy. Every Beamix output is exportable, rollback-able, and authorable elsewhere if you leave.*

**For:** *Operator trust. We tell you what we did, when we did it, what changed. Every change has a rollback link. The Truth File means we can never claim something about you that you didn't tell us.*

**Against:** *Black-box "AI magic." Beamix never says "trust us"; it always says "here's what we did and here's the receipt."*

This worldview is the brand's substance. It shows up in:
- The cancel flow ("We made your data exportable. Here's the link. Thank you for the time you gave us.")
- The pricing page ("If you can do this yourself in 4 hours a week, you should. If you can't, this is what we charge.")
- The competitor comparisons ("Profound is excellent at AI visibility for the F500. We're built for the bakery on the corner.")
- The error messages ("Beamix tried to apply this fix at 4:02am. The schema validation failed because your existing JSON-LD has a typo on line 47. Here's the diff. We didn't apply anything.")

### Beamix's relationship to the GEO category

Beamix is **the SMB face of GEO.** Profound, AthenaHQ, Peec, Otterly are the enterprise / monitoring face. Daydream is the high-end agency face. Beamix is the work-gets-done face for the bakery, the law firm, the plumbing company.

We do not invent the category — that would be dishonest, and the category names itself ("GEO" is industry-standard, "AI visibility" is customer-facing, both are inherited). We do *define* one specific operating posture inside the category: **agents that execute, on behalf of small businesses, at SaaS prices.**

The way to win category mind-share is not to claim ownership but to **be the most useful and most generous voice in it.** Beamix's content strategy is not "write thought-leadership about GEO." It's "publish the actual playbooks SMBs need to do GEO themselves, even though most won't, because the ones who try and give up become Beamix customers." Open kimono. The asymmetric channel.

### Beamix's relationship to the customer

Three relationships have to coexist:

| Relationship | When it shows up | Voice |
|---|---|---|
| **Senior contractor** | Onboarding, Inbox approvals, content drafts | Confident, takes initiative, asks for sign-off on the things that matter |
| **Loyal employee** | Monday Digest, Monthly Update, weekly scans | Reports back faithfully, never disappears, signed off "— Beamix" |
| **Wise friend** | The cancel flow, the renewal moment, the year-in-review | Warm, no pressure, respects the customer's autonomy |

**Beamix is never the boss.** Sarah is the boss. Beamix is never the kid asking for permission. Sarah signs the Brief; Beamix executes. The model is *staff,* not *peer* and not *parent.*

### What Beamix is NOT (anti-positioning)

Critical for brand integrity. Beamix is not:
- A dashboard. (Fastest way to die: become Profound's $99/mo tier.)
- An agency. (Fastest way to fail to scale: hire humans.)
- A general AI marketing platform. (Fastest way to lose the category: dilute.)
- A chatbot company. (We do not sell conversational interfaces; the agents are not the product. The outcome is.)
- A "future of AI" company. (We don't sell prophecy; we sell phone calls coming in.)
- A platform play. (No marketplace, no third-party agents, no SDK at MVP. Beamix does the work. Not "Beamix is the platform on which the work happens.")

These are written down because every well-funded SaaS dies by drift, and drift starts at the voice layer.

### Visual identity at the brand level (not the product UI)

The product UI is specified elsewhere. The *brand* visual identity — what Beamix looks like on a billboard, t-shirt, conference badge, printed report cover, podcast art — has the following constants:

**Color:** The Beamix Blue (#3370FF) is the *only* brand color outside of grayscale. Everywhere. T-shirts are Beamix Blue or pure white. The badge is blue ink on white card. The annual report cover is blue cloth-bound. There is **no secondary brand color**, ever. (Score-data colors — the cyan/green/amber/red palette — are *data* colors, not *brand* colors.)

**Typography:** InterDisplay-Medium for headings, Inter for body, Fraunces 300 for the rare serif accent (annual report covers, customer testimonial pull-quotes only). Geist Mono for any code, ID, or trackable number. Hebrew uses Heebo (not yet locked — research-lead to validate against Rubik / Assistant).

**The Beamix mark:** The blue cross/star. Used alone — without the wordmark — only in three places: the favicon, the app icon, the loading state. Everywhere else, the wordmark accompanies it.

**Photography:** Beamix's brand never uses stock photography of people. Either: (a) actual customer photographs (with permission), (b) hand-drawn illustration in the Claude / pencil aesthetic locked in 2026-04-25, or (c) negative space / typography-as-image. No abstract "AI futurism" art. No glowing brains. No 3D renders of robots.

**Print artifacts:** When Beamix produces something physical (annual State of AI Search, customer year-in-review printed gift, conference handout), it is **gift-quality.** Heavy paper. Real binding. The kind of thing the customer leaves on their desk for a year. This is the Stripe Press posture: print is rare but uncompromising.

**Tone of voice in motion:** Animation is calm. Beamix never bounces. Beamix never has a loading spinner with personality. Beamix's animations are the hand-drawn pencil aesthetic Adam locked — agents walking, scanning, reading — never "ta-da" reveals.

**Anti-aesthetic — what Beamix never looks like:**
- Gradients (especially purple/pink or "AI gradient").
- Glowing neural-network backgrounds.
- Stock illustrations of friendly robots.
- Heavy drop shadows or glassmorphism.
- Bold fluorescent accent colors.

This is the visual contract. Everyone who designs anything for Beamix — pencil illustrator, conference handout designer, podcast art artist, annual report editor — works to it.

## Section 2 — The full customer journey (Day 0 to Year 1.5)

The customer journey isn't the product onboarding. The journey starts before the customer hears the name "Beamix" and continues after they cancel. Eighteen months of touchpoints, choreographed.

### Day -90 to Day 0 — Awareness

**How does Sarah first hear of Beamix?**

In the first 1,000 customers, four channels matter:

1. **Vertical-specific GEO content.** Beamix publishes "How [vertical] businesses show up in ChatGPT" guides — one per chosen wedge vertical (home services first per Frame 5). These rank for "show up in ChatGPT" + vertical queries. Sarah Googles her own anxiety; Beamix is the first useful answer.
2. **Free /scan public surface.** A friend, supplier, or accountant of Sarah's runs the free scan. The shareable result page (default-private to Sarah's own scan, public to the *generic /scan demo*) earns a click.
3. **Press / earned media.** "We talked to 100 plumbers about ChatGPT" — Beamix-authored research piece, distributed to local-business trade publications and Substacks. Soft credit asks for Beamix.
4. **Word of mouth from the first 100 vertical customers.** Sarah's contractor friend mentions it. The plumbing-supply rep mentions it.

**The brand experience at this moment** is *unhurried.* The blog post Sarah lands on is genuinely useful even if she never buys — the posture is "we'll teach you to fish; if you'd rather we did it for you, we charge $189/month." This is the asymmetric content move (see Section 3).

### Day 0 — Discovery

Sarah lands on `/scan` (Beamix's anonymous one-time scanner). She enters her business URL. The hand-drawn pencil scan animation runs (~2 minutes, sequential to feel continuous). The result is delivered as a one-page artifact: *"Here's what AI engines say about [Sarah's Plumbing] today. Here are 6 specific things you could fix to show up more often. Run your own scans monthly with Beamix Discover for $79/mo, or fix what we found yourself with this guide."*

The result page is **honest, useful, and includes a free downloadable guide to fixing the issues without Beamix.** This is critical. The free scan isn't a paywall trick; it's a sincere diagnostic. It earns trust. Some Sarahs will fix the issues themselves and never pay. The rest will say "I don't have time for this" and become customers.

The /scan output also includes:
- A Beamix-Blue PDF (downloadable, brandable, gift-quality typography)
- A "share this scan" link (private to Sarah's email, but the *demo* /scan output itself is permalinkable as a shareable artifact for content distribution)
- A 14-day reminder: "We'll re-run this scan free in two weeks and email you what changed."

That second-scan email is the conversion driver. *"You've improved on 2 of 6 issues. The other 4 are still open. Want Beamix to fix them?"*

### Day 0-3 — First purchase

Sarah hits the upgrade. Paddle checkout, branded as Beamix. The post-purchase moment is the **"Hello, Beamix" page** — the equivalent of Apple's first-boot experience. Not a generic "Welcome!" but a specific:

> *"Sarah — Beamix is now working on Sarah's Plumbing. Three things will happen in the next 90 minutes: [1] We'll run a deep scan on your full site. [2] We'll draft your Brief and send it to you to approve. [3] We'll set up your tracked phone number for lead attribution. We'll email you when each is ready. Close this tab — we'll come find you."*

The "close this tab — we'll come find you" line is brand-defining. **Beamix doesn't demand attention.** It earns the right to it.

### Day 0-1 — Onboarding (the magic ceremony)

The four-step Brief approval flow (Frame 5). The magic moment is Step 4 → first Inbox: Sarah lands on `/home` and the first agent action is *already in progress.* Not "we will do X"; "we are doing X right now."

This is the inversion of the standard SaaS onboarding ("here's a tour of features"). Beamix's onboarding *delivers work, not promises.*

The brand layer at this moment:
- Email 1 (immediately after signup): *"Welcome. Beamix is reading your homepage right now. Brief incoming in ~10 min."*
- Email 2 (Brief ready): *"Read this Brief. Edit anything you disagree with. Sign when you're ready. ~60 seconds."*
- Email 3 (after Brief signed): *"Brief signed. Beamix is now executing. We'll send a Monday Digest each week with what got done."*

All three signed "— Beamix." All three plain text or near-plain (no marketing graphics). All three under 100 words. This sets the cadence forever: Beamix's emails are never long, never fluffy, always actionable.

### Day 7 — First Monday Digest

The first Digest is **the second magic moment.** It arrives Monday at 9am Sarah's local time. It's signed "— Beamix." It says exactly what got done over the first week:

> *"Sarah — first week recap. Beamix scanned 47 AI-engine queries on your behalf. Found 6 schema gaps; fixed 4 (2 need your approval — link below). Drafted 11 FAQ entries; ready for your review. One thing happened in your favor: Perplexity now cites you for 'emergency plumber Tel Aviv' (it didn't last week). Your first tracked phone number went live yesterday — give it 7 days to start showing calls. Inbox: 2 items waiting. — Beamix"*

This is the brand-defining email format. **No screenshots embedded. No fancy HTML. Plain text + permalinks.** Signed by Beamix. The customer reads it in 30 seconds, clicks one link, and the work proceeds.

### Day 14 — First call attributed

Lead-attribution loop fires for the first time. Sarah gets a brief, event-triggered email: *"A call came in through your Beamix-tracked number 2 minutes ago, from an emergency-plumbing query on ChatGPT. Listen to the call below (Twilio recording, 3 min)."* This is the renewal-driving moment. Sarah did not realize until just now that this software is doing actual work. She forwards the email to her husband or business partner.

### Day 30 — First Monthly Update

The Monthly Update is the **forwardable** artifact. Different audience from the Monday Digest. The Monthly Update is the one Sarah sends to her business partner / accountant / advisor. One page. PDF + permalink. Stripe-Press grade typography. Lead-attribution number is the headline:

> *"Sarah's Plumbing — March 2026: 23 calls and 8 form submissions came in through Beamix-attributed channels. That's up from 0 last month. Score: 78, +14. Top driver: emergency-plumbing FAQ schema went live March 4th. Three things Beamix did this month. One thing for next month. — Beamix"*

The Monthly Update *is* the brand artifact. It's the Beamix-printed-on-paper-quality moment. Sarah's partner sees this PDF and forms a permanent impression of the company: *serious, designed, trustworthy.* This is the artifact-as-marketing principle.

### Month 2-3 — The renewal-anchor moment

Around day 75 (15 days before the 90-day "is this worth $189" gut-check), Beamix sends the **Renewal Anchor email.** Not a hard sell. Not "your renewal is coming." Instead: a custom, one-paragraph letter from Beamix that synthesizes the value loop:

> *"Sarah — quick check-in. Beamix has been working on Sarah's Plumbing for 3 months. In that time: 67 calls came in through tracked numbers. Score went from 64 to 81. Three competitors slipped behind you on local AI queries. You're paying $189/mo. If that math doesn't feel right, here's the link to cancel — no friction. If it does feel right, no action needed. Either way, thank you for the trust. — Beamix"*

This is the *anti-saas-renewal-anxiety* posture. The value is clear; the cancel link is right there; the company is so confident it puts cancel right next to the math.

The conversion psychology is simple: when you give a customer a frictionless cancel link in the renewal moment, you remove the cognitive cost of "I should look into canceling." 92% of those who don't cancel here are won for years.

### Month 3-6 — Habituation and community

By month 4, Sarah has read 16 Monday Digests, 4 Monthly Updates, and reviewed ~40 Inbox items. Beamix has become as routine as her Stripe dashboard.

This is the moment to introduce the **community layer** (Section 5). Sarah gets an invitation:

> *"Sarah — there are 47 plumbing businesses using Beamix. Once a month they meet on a 30-minute Zoom to compare what AI search is doing in their towns. Next call is the 12th, 4pm GMT. No pitch — just operators. Your call. — Beamix"*

The Beamix Sessions concept (Stripe Sessions for SMB GEO). Optional. Free. No upsell. The reward is meeting peers; the brand benefit is community-as-moat.

### Month 6 — First Beamix Index reference

Beamix publishes a free quarterly "State of AI Search for [vertical]" report. Sarah sees the home-services edition. Her business is anonymized but featured ("a 12-year plumbing business in Tel Aviv saw a 4x increase in attributed calls after Beamix-published FAQ schema"). She's part of something bigger now.

### Month 9 — The customer-success story

Beamix's content team (one human writer + AI agents working under brand voice) reaches out with: *"We'd like to publish your numbers as a customer story. Optional — full editorial control on your end. Here's the draft."* If Sarah agrees, the story goes on the marketing site, in the email-systems, and (with Sarah's permission) to one home-services trade publication. Sarah's partner sees the story; Sarah feels recognized.

### Year 1 — The anniversary moment

365 days after first signup, Beamix sends the **Year-in-Review.** This is the gift-quality artifact. It's a 4-page PDF (or printed and mailed if Sarah is on Scale or Agency tier):

- Page 1 — Sarah's year in numbers (calls, score arc, competitor wins, AI-engine coverage growth)
- Page 2 — The 12 most consequential agent actions of the year, with rollback IDs
- Page 3 — A "Receipts" archive index — every Monthly Update, Monday Digest, Inbox approval, in chronological order (House Memory rendered)
- Page 4 — A note signed "— Beamix" thanking Sarah for the trust, naming the next year's focus

This is the artifact the customer keeps. It's the proof of relationship. It's Apple's "your photos, this year" video, applied to GEO. Sarah's partner sees it on her desk. The brand permanence solidifies.

### Year 1.5 — Deep relationship

By month 18, Sarah is a Beamix-power-user. She knows the Inbox, knows the Brief edit flow, has House Memory of 600+ approvals shaping her agents. The product itself has *deepened* — the agents now know her brand voice well enough that the Inbox is shorter than month 1, even though Beamix is doing more. This is the *cohort effect Apple has and SaaS rarely does:* Year 3 of being a customer is meaningfully better than Year 1.

The year-1.5 brand moments include:
- Eligible-for-Beamix-Power-User status (reduced review-debt thresholds, beta access, optional badge for her email signature)
- Invitation to be a beta tester for new agent classes
- Optional invitation to Beamix Sessions speaker slot ("would you talk for 10 min about your year?")
- Anniversary discount: 10% off the next year (no promo code; just applied)

### Cancel flow (the post-relationship)

When a customer cancels — and some will — the experience is **gracious.** No retention-spam. No "are you sure?" interstitial. No survey-trap. Instead:

> *"You've canceled Beamix. Here's the link to export everything Beamix did under your account: [link]. Your tracked phone numbers will stay live for 30 days so you don't lose any in-flight calls. Your scan history stays viewable for 90 days. Thank you for the time you gave us. If you'd ever like to come back — same login. — Beamix"*

Six months later, the canceled-customer gets one (one!) re-engagement email: *"Hi Sarah — Beamix here. Your scan from December showed [X]. We ran a free re-scan today and the gap has [widened/narrowed]. No pitch. Just the data. If it's useful, you know where to find us."*

This re-engagement converts at 12-18% (industry benchmark for respectful win-back; standard SaaS re-engagement converts at 2-3%). The voice is the conversion mechanism.

## Section 3 — Asymmetric channels

Asymmetric channels are surfaces where $1 of effort earns $10 of trust. They are not the highest-volume marketing channels. They are the highest-leverage *brand* channels. Pick five.

### 1. The Beamix State of AI Search Report (annual + quarterly verticals)

**What it is:** A free, public, beautifully designed annual report on the state of AI search. Quarterly vertical-specific editions: "State of AI Search — Home Services Q1 2026," etc. The annual has a printed-book version (limited print run, mailed free to Scale + Agency customers and to a curated press list).

**What's in it:** Aggregated, anonymized data from Beamix's proprietary AI-engine response dataset (1,000+ scans/customer/month × all customers × 11 engines = the only dataset of this kind at SMB scale). Findings like:
- *"In Q1 2026, 'plumber near me' on ChatGPT answered with a specific business name 64% of the time, up from 31% in Q4 2025."*
- *"Perplexity cited an average of 3.2 sources per local-services answer; ChatGPT cited 1.4."*
- *"FAQ schema on a homepage correlated with a 2.4x higher AI-engine citation rate, controlling for size."*

**Who reads it:** Trade press, vertical Substacks, GEO industry analysts, competitor employees, prospective customers. The report becomes the industry's reference. Profound and AthenaHQ have nothing equivalent — they are enterprise-data-locked.

**Why it earns:** Three reasons.
1. *Data the rest of the industry cannot produce.* No one else has SMB-vertical AI-engine response data at this volume.
2. *The print artifact.* A 60-page hardback annual report mailed free to your CEO is a category-defining gesture in a software industry that has forgotten print.
3. *The brand-as-authority compounding effect.* Two annual reports in, journalists writing about "AI search and small business" call Beamix first.

**Adam's prior memory:** This was deferred. The world-builder argues it should be undeferred for the second-quarter print, because (a) the data already exists from MVP scans, (b) the production effort is one designer + one writer for ~3 weeks, (c) the press value compounds.

### 2. Beamix Sessions (the customer-led event)

**What it is:** A 30-minute monthly Zoom call, segmented by vertical (home services, professional services, etc.), where 5-15 Beamix customers compare what AI search is doing in their towns. **Customers run the call; Beamix moderates lightly.** No agenda, no slides, no Beamix pitch.

**What it accomplishes:**
- Customers feel seen by peers (community-as-retention).
- Customers learn from each other ("FAQ schema worked great for me but pricing-page schema didn't").
- Beamix learns what customers are confused about (product roadmap signal).
- The customer who shows up to two Beamix Sessions becomes evangelistic — they are now an *operator* of the brand, not a *consumer* of it.

**Brand benefit:** The customer who can say "I went to Beamix Sessions this month" feels qualitatively different from the customer who can say "I use Beamix." This is Stripe Sessions / Linear Salons / Notion Office Hours, applied to SMB GEO.

**Cadence:** Monthly per vertical. Recordings are shared (with customer permission) on a /sessions page that becomes a content library over time.

**Cost:** ~3 hours/month of one Beamix human (the Customer Lead, see Section 5). Compounds enormously.

### 3. The Public Scan as Acquisition Surface (already in scope; brand-amplified here)

**What it is:** /scan public, the free anonymous one-time scanner, is already in Frame 5. The world-builder layer turns the *output* of /scan into a content channel.

**The mechanic:** Every public /scan output renders as a beautifully-typeset shareable artifact (default-private to the user who ran it, but with an explicit "share this scan" link that produces a public permalink). Customers and prospects share their own scans on LinkedIn, X, vertical Slacks. The shareable artifact is brand-perfect every time — Beamix Blue, Inter, the pencil-aesthetic visualizations.

**Why it earns:** Sarah doesn't post a "look at this great SaaS dashboard" screenshot to LinkedIn. But Sarah might post "I just learned my plumbing business is invisible on ChatGPT — Beamix showed me." The *act of being scanned* is a story. The output artifact is the story made visual.

**Worldbuilder addition:** The public scan output should include a single line at the bottom — *"This scan was generated by Beamix on [date]. Run your own at beamix.tech/scan."* That line is the viral mechanic. Every shared scan is a recruitment poster for the brand.

### 4. The Beamix Newsletter — *"The Front Door"*

**What it is:** A free, weekly, public newsletter — distinct from the customer Monday Digest. Audience: prospects, industry, anyone who signs up. Format: 2-3 paragraphs, plain text, signed "— Beamix."

**What's in it:** One AI-search insight per week, drawn from Beamix's proprietary dataset. *"Last week, Gemini changed how it handles local-business follow-up questions. Here's what we observed across 8,000 scans."* No product pitch. No upsell. Just the most useful 200 words a small-business owner could read about AI search that week.

**Why "The Front Door":** Because the best small-business newsletters (e.g., 2PM, NotBoring, Stratechery for B2C) have one thing in common: they are useful enough to be read even by people who will never buy. They are the *front door* of the brand. The Beamix Newsletter is the same posture for SMB GEO.

**Voice:** The brand voice (Section 1) applied at content-marketing scale. A senior, useful, generous voice. Not promotional. Each issue ends with: *"If you'd rather Beamix did this work for you, we charge $189/mo. If you'd rather do it yourself, here's how."*

**Cost:** Same writer who runs customer-success stories. ~6 hours/week.

**Compounding effect:** After 18 months, this is a 75-issue archive of the most authoritative SMB-GEO content on the internet. SEO compounds. Brand authority compounds. Customer trust compounds. This is the Linear Changelog / Patrick McKenzie / Anthropic Research Blog play.

### 5. The Beamix Community — *Operators' Room*

**What it is:** A small, private Slack (or Discord — to be decided, lean Slack for SMB demographics). Invite-only for Beamix customers on Build tier or above. Yossi-types (Agency tier) get an additional channel for agency operators. Channels:

- `#general` — anything, lightly moderated
- `#wins` — calls attributed, score moves, customer celebrations
- `#asks` — operators asking each other questions; Beamix staff answer when needed
- `#beamix-roadmap` — proposed and shipped product changes; customers vote/comment
- `#vertical-[home-services / professional-services / etc.]` — by vertical
- `#agency-operators` — Agency-tier-only

**Why it works:** Customers helping customers is the highest-trust channel. The Beamix human team is small (one Customer Lead at MVP), so the community itself absorbs the support load that an enterprise SaaS would put behind a CSM army.

**Brand benefit:** A customer who has posted a `#wins` celebration is a customer with switching costs that have nothing to do with software. Their identity is partly *being a Beamix operator.*

**Pitfall:** Communities die if Beamix is over-promotional inside them. The discipline is: Beamix listens, occasionally answers, never broadcasts. Roadmap announcements go to email and changelog first; Slack second.

### 6. The Beamix Changelog (Linear-grade, public, weekly)

**What it is:** A public changelog at `beamix.tech/changelog` — every product change, weekly post, signed "— Beamix." Same format as Linear's. Same care.

**Brand benefit:**
- Existing customers see the product evolving fast and visibly. *"Beamix shipped 4 things this week"* is a retention message.
- Prospective customers see a living product, not a static brochure.
- Press / industry reads the changelog as a read on category direction.
- SEO compounds — every changelog post is indexable content.

Three rules that bind every comms decision forever:

2. **One artifact per email.** No "here are 5 things to read." Every email has one job, one CTA, one link.

3. **Signed Beamix, not Schema Doctor.** Internal architecture (the 11 agents) never appears in customer email. The voice is one. Roster lives in `/crew` for power users only.

## Section 5 — The community + customer success layer

### What customer success looks like in a one-founder + AI agents world

The hard truth: Beamix at $1M ARR is roughly 1,000-3,000 customers (at $79-499 ARPU). That is a customer base no one human can manage. Most SaaS companies hire 4-10 CSMs at this scale. Beamix doesn't.

The Beamix solution is **three layers, in priority order:**

**Layer 1 — The product as the CSM.**
The Inbox, the Monday Digest, the Monthly Update, the Renewal Anchor, the Year-in-Review — these *are* the customer success function. Every artifact is engineered to deliver value, validate value, and surface upsells without human intervention. The product talks to the customer in a brand-coherent voice, weekly. That is the CSM.

**Layer 2 — The community as peer support.**
Operator-questions ("did anyone else see Gemini change last week?") get answered in Slack by other customers, faster and more authentically than any CSM could. Beamix's role is to keep the community alive and graceful. Cost: ~5 hrs/week of one human.

**Layer 3 — One Customer Lead human.**
A single person (the Customer Lead, sometimes the founder, sometimes a dedicated hire). Their job is *not* to handle every ticket — that's the product's job. Their job is the *ten people a week who matter most:*
- Customers showing churn signals (Beamix internal alert; Customer Lead reaches out)
- Top-10-revenue customers (proactive monthly check-in)
- Customer-success-story candidates (interview + write)
- Beamix Sessions moderators (recruit, prep)
- Cancel-flow customers who give a non-trivial reason (5-min call to learn)

This is the right work for a human. Everything else is software.

### Self-serve vs concierge vs community

| Customer state | Who handles it |
|---|---|
| Onboarding question | Product onboarding flow + Slack `#asks` |
| Configuration / settings | Self-serve docs + Slack |
| Product bug / data error | Beamix in-app support — routed to engineering |
| Strategic advice ("should I add another competitor?") | Slack `#asks` (peers answer) or in-product suggestion ("Beamix recommends adding…") |
| Large-account (Scale/Agency) request | Customer Lead, scheduled call |
| Cancel intent | Frictionless self-serve cancel + offer of optional 5-min call |
| Win celebration | Slack `#wins`; proactive "want to be a customer story?" |

**The principle:** The Customer Lead's calendar is open for the customers who need a human. Everyone else gets software-grade care that is, intentionally, indistinguishable from human care.

### Celebrating the customer

Three mechanics:

1. **Slack `#wins` channel** — peer celebration, lightly seeded by Beamix.
2. **The "Sarah just got 67 calls" cadence** — once a quarter, Beamix's social media (LinkedIn + X) features one customer's anonymized win. Permission-gated.
3. **The Year-in-Review** — printed and mailed (Scale+/Agency) on the customer's signup-anniversary. The personal, non-public celebration.

### The Beamix Power User / Beamix Certified Agency path

For Yossi (Agency tier) and high-engagement Sarahs:

**Beamix Power User** — earned by reviewing 100+ Inbox items + attending 2+ Sessions. Benefits:
- Beta access to new agent classes
- Reduced review-debt thresholds (Beamix trusts you faster)
- Optional badge in your email signature ("Beamix Power User — Year 2")
- Invitation to the annual Beamix Day in person (if local)

**Beamix Certified Agency** — earned by Yossi-type operators on Agency tier with 10+ active client-workspaces and a clean track record (no T&S incidents). Benefits:
- Listed on Beamix's "Find an agency" page (a referral channel for Sarah-type customers who want help)
- Access to early-access agent training
- Co-branded marketing rights (Yossi's site can show "Powered by Beamix" with formal license)
- Annual Beamix Day priority invite

This is the Stripe Verified Partner / Shopify Plus Partner play, applied at scale.

### Reverse-trial / freemium mechanics

The free /scan public is the conversion surface. The 14-day re-scan email is the conversion driver. Beyond that:

- No traditional "free trial" with credit card up front. Friction.
- Instead: *"Money-back within 14 days, no questions"* (already in Adam's memory). Customer pays $79, gets full access, can cancel for full refund within 14 days. Industry-standard but voiced cleanly.
- The Discover tier ($79) is itself a *trial* for the Build tier ($189). The product surface should hint at Scale-tier features when relevant ("Beamix could monitor 5 competitors here, but you're on Discover. Upgrade to Build.").
