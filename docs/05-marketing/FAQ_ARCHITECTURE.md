# Beamix — FAQ Architecture
*40 questions organized by customer stage. Built for FAQPage JSON-LD AND LLM extraction.*
*v1.0 · 2026-04-14 · Owner: Growth Lead*

---

## Design principles

Every answer in this document is written to satisfy two requirements simultaneously:

1. **FAQPage JSON-LD extraction** — each answer is self-contained, starts with a definition or direct statement, and is 40-80 words. Google reads these for AI Overviews.
2. **LLM passage extraction** — definition-first structure, one fact per sentence, no hedging, no "it depends." Perplexity and ChatGPT lift passages that can stand alone as answers.

Answers are intentionally short and dense. The goal is not to be comprehensive inside the answer — it's to be citable. Long-form explanation belongs in the linked blog article.

## Hub 1 — GEO Basics FAQ
**Framer page:** `/faq/geo-basics`
**Meta title:** "GEO Basics FAQ — What Is AI Search Visibility? | Beamix"

**Q1: What is AI search visibility?**
AI search visibility is a measure of how often and how prominently a business appears in answers generated by AI engines — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Claude — when users ask questions about products or services in that category. Unlike traditional search rankings, AI engines synthesize answers from multiple sources rather than listing URLs. A business with high AI search visibility gets mentioned by name in those synthesized answers.

*→ Blog cross-reference: Pillar article #1 — "The SMB Owner's Complete Guide to AI Search Visibility"*

---

**Q2: What is GEO — generative engine optimization?**
GEO stands for generative engine optimization. It is the practice of structuring content so that AI engines — like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity — include your business or website in their generated answers. GEO techniques include adding statistics, expert quotations, inline source citations, and structured data markup to existing content. A 2024 Princeton study (Aggarwal et al.) found that these techniques increase visibility in generative answers by up to 41%.

*→ Blog cross-reference: Pillar article #1*

---

**Q3: How is AI search different from Google search?**
Google search returns a ranked list of links. AI search engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini — synthesize a direct answer from multiple sources and cite the ones they used. You do not show up as a link; you show up as a named recommendation or cited source inside the answer. This means ranking in AI search requires different techniques than traditional SEO — content structure, schema markup, and authoritative signals matter more than keyword density.

*→ Blog cross-reference: Pillar article #1*

---

**Q4: Do my Google rankings affect my AI search visibility?**
Yes, partially. Google AI Overviews use Google's own search index, so strong Google rankings help with AI Overviews. ChatGPT uses Bing's index for live web search, so Bing ranking also matters. Perplexity runs real-time web search and favors authoritative, frequently-updated, topically-specialized pages. A business can rank well in AI search without ranking at the top of traditional Google results — but AI visibility and Google visibility reinforce each other.

*→ Blog cross-reference: GBP + ChatGPT checklist article*

---

**Q5: Why should a small business care about AI search in 2026?**
AI search converts at approximately 30% higher rates than traditional web search, based on a 2025 study of AI-referred traffic. Google AI Overviews now appear on more than 50% of queries. Early citations in AI search compound: when an AI engine cites a business repeatedly, that pattern reinforces itself in future model outputs. Small businesses that establish AI search visibility now are building an advantage that becomes harder for competitors to close each quarter.

*→ Blog cross-reference: Pillar article #1, AI invisibility cost calculator*

---

**Q6: Which AI engines should a small business focus on?**
The four AI engines with the highest business impact for SMBs in 2026 are: ChatGPT (uses Bing's index for live queries), Google AI Overviews (uses Google's index + Gemini), Perplexity (real-time web search with inline citations), and Claude (live search added 2024-2025). Each engine has different source preferences and retrieval mechanisms. A complete AI search strategy addresses all four rather than optimizing for one.

*→ Blog cross-reference: Pillar article #1*

---

**Q7: What does "getting cited by ChatGPT" actually mean for my business?**
When a user asks ChatGPT a question like "What are the best insurance agents in Tel Aviv?", ChatGPT searches Bing's index, retrieves candidate pages, and synthesizes an answer naming specific businesses. Getting cited means your business name appears in that answer as a recommended option. In practice, this means customers who never visit Google see your business recommended before they visit any website. It is the top-of-funnel equivalent of a trusted friend's referral.

*→ Blog cross-reference: Law firm vertical article, restaurant vertical article*

---

**Q8: How long does it take to see results from GEO optimization?**
Most businesses see first improvements in Google AI Overviews within 4-8 weeks of implementing structured data (FAQPage schema, Article schema) and publishing updated content with statistics and citations. Perplexity and ChatGPT citation improvements typically take 6-12 weeks as crawlers re-index updated content. Beamix's scan engine measures your baseline and tracks changes weekly so you can see the direction of movement before results become consistent.

*→ Blog cross-reference: 60-day checklist article*

Restaurants improve ChatGPT visibility by optimizing four signals: Google Business Profile completeness (hours, menu, photos), review volume and recency on Google and Yelp (AI engines read reviews), schema markup (Restaurant type with cuisine, price range, and location data), and FAQ content addressing common queries ("best Italian restaurant near [neighborhood]"). **Q30: Why do law firms need to optimize for AI search?**
When someone asks ChatGPT "which employment lawyer in Tel Aviv handles non-compete disputes?", ChatGPT synthesizes an answer citing specific firms. Law firms that appear in those answers get client inquiries without requiring the client to perform a Google search. Legal queries on AI search have grown significantly as users trust AI engines for initial professional referrals. **Q31: How do insurance agents rank on Perplexity?**
Perplexity favors pages that are authoritative for a specific topic, frequently updated, and topically scoped. **Q32: Do moving companies show up on ChatGPT for local searches?**
Moving companies appear in ChatGPT local search results when they have strong Bing search presence (ChatGPT uses Bing's index for live queries), Google Business Profile completeness, and content that answers specific moving questions. "Best moving companies in [city]" queries return AI-synthesized recommendations. **Q33: How do dentists appear in Google AI Overviews?**
Dentists appear in Google AI Overviews by ranking well in traditional Google search (AI Overviews pull from Google's index), maintaining complete Google Business Profile data (especially services, hours, and accepted insurance), publishing FAQ content about dental procedures, and implementing LocalBusiness + Dentist schema with MedicalSpecialty attributes. Patient-facing educational content — "how long does a crown take?" — is particularly effective because it matches the question format AI Overviews favor.

**Q35: What is FAQPage schema and why does it matter for AI search?**
FAQPage schema is a structured data format (JSON-LD) that explicitly marks up question-and-answer content on a web page so search engines and AI engines can extract it cleanly. Pages with FAQPage schema are 3.2× more likely to appear in Google AI Overviews than pages without it, based on independent measurement. Beamix's Schema Optimizer agent generates ready-to-publish FAQPage JSON-LD for any FAQ content your business publishes.

*→ Blog cross-reference: FAQPage schema article*

---

**Q36: How does Beamix scan AI search engines?**
Beamix queries each AI engine with real prompts constructed from your business's industry, location, and service keywords — the same queries your customers type. Results are recorded and scored: whether your business is mentioned, at what position, with what sentiment. Scans run on a schedule (weekly, every 3 days, or daily depending on plan) and manually on demand. Beamix does not use simulated or cached data — each scan is a live query at the time it runs.

---

**Q37: Does Beamix integrate with my website CMS?**
Beamix produces content files in formats compatible with all major CMS platforms: Markdown for blog posts, JSON-LD for schema files, and plain text for FAQ content. Beamix does not auto-publish to your CMS — you receive the content file and publish it yourself. Direct CMS integrations (WordPress, Webflow, Framer) are on the roadmap for 2026.

---

**Q38: What is llms.txt and should my business have one?**
llms.txt is a proposed plain-text file at your site root that describes your site's content for AI engines. As of April 2026, no major AI platform has confirmed they read it — server log audits show zero crawls of llms.txt by GPTBot, ClaudeBot, or PerplexityBot. Beamix recommends implementing it as a low-cost signal of best-practice intent, but it is not a primary AI search strategy. Robots.txt configuration allowing AI search crawlers matters significantly more.

*→ Blog cross-reference: FRAMER_SEO.md (technical setup, not published as blog post)*

---

**Q39: Does Beamix help with Google Business Profile optimization?**
Beamix does not connect directly to your Google Business Profile or manage it. However, Beamix's Content Writer and FAQ Agent produce content and FAQ pages that, when published on your website, strengthen the entity signals AI engines use when evaluating your business — including signals that complement GBP data. Beamix also provides recommendations about GBP completeness based on your scan results.

*→ Blog cross-reference: GBP + ChatGPT checklist article*

---

**Q40: Is my scan data private?**
Scan data in Beamix is private to your account. Beamix does not share individual business scan results with other users or publish them externally. Beamix may use aggregated, anonymized data across multiple businesses (for example, in the Beamix Visibility Index data reports) — but no individual business data is identifiable in that reporting. You can delete your account and all associated scan data at any time from account settings.
