# Competitive Content Gaps — GEO/AI-Search SMB Niche
*Research date: 2026-04-14 · Owner: Research Lead*

## TL;DR

1. **The GEO content market is dominated by enterprise/agency-targeted content.** Profound, AthenaHQ, Writesonic and Surfer SEO write to SaaS marketing teams and agencies — not SMB owners. Beamix's largest opportunity is the SMB owner-operator angle.
2. **Vertical guides for local-SMB verticals are nearly empty.** "How restaurants/law firms/movers/insurance agents/dentists rank on ChatGPT" is wide-open territory. Existing coverage is generic ("how SMBs rank on ChatGPT"), not vertical-specific.
3. **Hebrew/Israeli market = zero competition.** No GEO content vendor publishes in Hebrew. Beamix can own this category by default.
4. **Saturated topics (do not waste cycles):** "What is GEO?" "GEO vs SEO," "How to optimize for ChatGPT 101," "Best GEO tools" lists. Every competitor has covered these 5+ times.
5. **The "done for you" framing is missing.** All competitors sell dashboards/insights. Nobody is publishing case studies of "we ran the agent — here's what shipped." Beamix's product positioning IS the content gap.
6. **Format winners across competitors:** (a) data reports with original research, (b) tool comparison pages with comparison tables, (c) "ultimate guide" pillar pages 3,000-5,000 words, (d) interview-style posts with named experts.
7. **Profound is the content leader by quality and frequency** — they've turned their citation analytics into a content moat (publishing original citation studies that get cited back).
8. **Cost/ROI calculator pages are missing across the niche.** Strong opportunity for Beamix: a "What does AI invisibility cost your business?" interactive tool.
9. **Local SEO + AI search intersection** is barely covered. Combine "GBP optimization" + "ChatGPT visibility" content — Beamix's vertical SMBs care about both.
10. **First 25 article ideas (full list in §7)** prioritize: vertical-specific guides (8 articles), original Beamix data reports (4), comparison/tool pages (5), Hebrew/Israeli market (3), tactical how-to (5).

## 1. Competitor Profiles

### Profound (tryprofound.com/blog)
- **Publishing cadence:** ~weekly
- **Voice:** Data-driven, analyst, slightly enterprise
- **Format strengths:** Original citation studies (e.g., "AI Platform Citation Patterns" — heavily cited across the niche), tool comparison pages
- **Topics owned:** Citation share-of-voice methodology, multi-engine citation comparison, GEO measurement, enterprise GEO
- **Top posts (observed):**
  - "AI Platform Citation Patterns: How ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity Source Information"
  - "Best Generative Engine Optimization Tools for AI in 2026"
- **Voice:** Authoritative, citation-heavy, data-first
- **Funding context:** $20M Series A June 2025 (Kleiner Perkins) — well-resourced content team
- **Gap:** Almost no SMB-specific or local-vertical content

### Otterly.ai (otterly.ai/blog)
- **Cadence:** Monthly
- **Voice:** Approachable, beginner-friendly
- **Format:** How-to guides, simple explainers
- **Topics owned:** "GEO basics for non-technical users," mention tracking explainers
- **Voice:** Friendly, less data-heavy
- **Gap:** Light publishing volume, light original research

### AthenaHQ (athenahq.ai/blog, athenahq.ai/articles)
- **Cadence:** Weekly+
- **Voice:** Marketing strategy, mid-market
- **Format:** Long-form playbooks, "blueprint" framing
- **Top posts:**
  - "The Ultimate Guide to AI Marketing Platforms in 2025"
  - "The 2025 Playbook for AI-Driven Marketing Success" (GEO Marketing Blueprint)
- **Topics owned:** GEO playbooks, schema markup at scale, entity tagging
- **Voice:** Strategic, blueprint-heavy
- **Gap:** Aimed at mid-market marketing teams, not SMBs

### Writesonic (writesonic.com/blog — GEO content)
- **Cadence:** High-volume (multiple per week)
- **Voice:** Generic SEO content with GEO bolt-on
- **Format:** Heavy on listicles + "alternatives" pages (e.g., "9 Best Profound Alternatives," "7 Best AthenaHQ Alternatives")
- **Topics owned:** Comparison pages targeting competitor brand keywords
- **Voice:** Workmanlike, search-optimized, formulaic
- **Gap:** Quality is meh; opportunity to outclass on craft

### Surfer SEO (surferseo.com/blog)
- **Cadence:** High-volume
- **Voice:** Practitioner SEO, broad audience
- **Top topics:** AI detection avoidance, content optimization, NLP-driven SEO, AI Overviews tactics
- **Format:** How-tos, sometimes data-driven
- **Gap:** Not GEO-native; AI search is a side topic, not core

### HubSpot (blog.hubspot.com — AI search coverage)
- **Cadence:** Daily across all blog channels
- **Format:** Comparison pages, ultimate guides, news
- **Top posts on GEO:** "Profound vs. AthenaHQ" (comparison)
- **Voice:** Inbound marketing classic — broad, accessible
- **Gap:** Generalist; doesn't go deep on GEO mechanics

### SE Ranking (seranking.com/blog)
- **Cadence:** Weekly
- **Voice:** SEO practitioner, data-driven
- **Format:** Tool/comparison content, original research (e.g., 300k-domain llms.txt audit)
- **Topics owned:** SEO measurement, AI search measurement, ranking factor studies
- **Voice:** Technical, study-driven
- **Gap:** SEO-first not GEO-first; SMB angle thin

### Semrush (semrush.com/blog — AI Toolkit blog)
- **Cadence:** Multiple per day
- **Format:** Ultimate guides, tool walkthroughs, original studies
- **Topics owned:** Massive SEO breadth; AI Toolkit content is newer
- **Voice:** Professional, authoritative
- **Gap:** Big-brand voice; not SMB-empathetic

### Ahrefs (ahrefs.com/blog)
- **Cadence:** Weekly
- **Voice:** Tim Soulo / Patrick Stox — practitioner-led, occasional contrarian
- **Topics owned:** Link building, technical SEO, original research
- **GEO coverage:** Sporadic but high-quality
- **Gap:** GEO isn't central to their content strategy yet

## 5. Format Patterns That Work (steal these)

- **Data report with original numbers** (Profound model) — single-source authority signal, gets cited by everyone in the niche
- **Comparison table with verdict** (HubSpot model) — wins comparison-keyword traffic
- **"Ultimate guide" pillar with linked sub-articles** (AthenaHQ model) — semantic completeness for AI Overviews
- **Interview/expert quote post** — adds Quotation Addition (Princeton GEO +28%)
- **Case study with named client + screenshots + dates** — unbeatable E-E-A-T
- **Listicle with strong opinion** (Ahrefs model) — better than bland "10 things" listicles
- **Calculator / interactive tool** — link magnet + dwell-time signal
