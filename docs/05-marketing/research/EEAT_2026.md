# E-E-A-T in 2026 — Post-HCU Reality
*Research date: 2026-04-14 · Owner: Research Lead*

## TL;DR

1. Google's most recent **Search Quality Rater Guidelines** were updated **September 11, 2025** (currently live PDF at guidelines.raterhub.com). The update added **AI Overview rating examples** and **broadened YMYL** to include government, elections, and civic processes. [Source 1, 2]
2. **E-E-A-T expanded beyond YMYL.** The December 2025 core update extended E-E-A-T scrutiny to "practically all competitive searches" — e-commerce reviews, SaaS comparisons, how-to guides. There is no longer a "safe non-YMYL zone." [Source 3]
3. **Lily Ray's HCU autopsy of 500 hit sites** (the most-cited primary research): 82% had thin content (<1,000 words), 74% lacked author bylines, 68% had minimal internal linking, 61% showed scaled publishing with minimal QC. [Source 4]
4. **Recovery pattern:** Sites that rebuilt comprehensive topic coverage recovered ~3× faster than those polishing individual articles. Build a topical fortress, not a content patchwork. [Source 4]
5. **Author bios + Person schema are the single highest-leverage E-E-A-T fix** an SMB blog can make. Cheap, fast, measurable. [Source 5, 6]
6. **Google's official AI content stance (unchanged since Feb 2023):** "We focus on the quality of content, not how content is produced." (Danny Sullivan). However, March 2024 spam policy update added **"Scaled Content Abuse"** — penalizes mass-produced low-value content regardless of human/AI origin. [Source 7]
7. **First-hand Experience signals (the second 'E')** are now demonstrably weighted: original photos with EXIF data, "we tested" framing, named clients in case studies, sample data, screenshots of tools used.
8. **YMYL definition (2025):** health, finance, safety, legal, **government/elections/civic** (new), and any topic that could "significantly impact a person's wellbeing." Beamix content sometimes brushes YMYL when discussing pricing/ROI claims for SMBs.
9. **Trust is the most important of E-E-A-T** per the QRG itself — "Untrustworthy pages have low E-E-A-T no matter how Experienced, Expert, or Authoritative they may seem." Translation: HTTPS, About page, contact, business address, citations to authoritative sources are non-negotiable.
10. **HCU recoveries in June 2025 core update** — first significant restorations after ~22 months. The pattern: comprehensive topical authority + author entity strengthening + content pruning of thin pages. [Source 8]

## 1. Current Google Guidance (Primary Sources)

### Search Quality Rater Guidelines (Sept 11, 2025 version)
- **Live PDF:** https://guidelines.raterhub.com/searchqualityevaluatorguidelines.pdf
- **Length:** 176+ pages
- **Section structure most relevant to Beamix:**
  - §3 Page Quality Rating — uses E-E-A-T to set rating
  - §3.4 E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust) — Trust is the "most important member"
  - §3.5 YMYL — definition expanded 2025 to include civic processes
  - §3.6+ Lowest Quality Pages — flags for harmful, deceptive, untrustworthy content
  - New 2025 sections: AI Overview rating examples
- **Trust hierarchy quoted in QRG:** "Untrustworthy pages have low E-E-A-T no matter how Experienced, Expert, or Authoritative they may seem."

### Google Search Central — Helpful Content Guidance
- **Live page:** https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content
- "Self-assess content" questions: Does content provide original info? Substantial value? First-hand expertise?
- AI content stance: focus on quality, not method, but penalize content "primarily made for search engines."

### Lessons from the Core Update Cadence (2024-2026)
- March 2024: Helpful Content System merged into core ranking. Spam policy added "Scaled Content Abuse," "Site Reputation Abuse," "Expired Domain Abuse." [Source 7]
- August 2024 core update: "Now, we very much have heard from creators that have been working hard, that have been following our advice, that they're still not seeing improvement."
- **June 2025 core update:** First meaningful HCU recoveries observed. [Source 8]
- **December 2025 core update:** Major expansion of E-E-A-T scrutiny beyond YMYL into all competitive searches. [Source 3]

| Update | Date | What was hit | Recovery pattern |

| **August 2024 core** | Aug 2024 | Continued pressure on HCU losers; some independent creators recovered partially | Author-driven sites with first-person experience |
| **March 2025 core** | Mar 2025 | Spam policy enforcement on AI-scaled sites | — |
| **June 2025 core** | Jun 2025 | **First major HCU recovery wave.** Independent creators regained visibility | Comprehensive topical coverage + author entity strengthening |
| **December 2025 core** | Dec 2025 | E-E-A-T extended to non-YMYL competitive queries | Sites with weak Trust signals across e-commerce, SaaS comparison, how-to all hit |

[Source 4, 8, 3]

### Lily Ray's HCU Loser Patterns (n=500 sites)
- 82% had thin content (<1,000 words on majority of pages)
- 74% lacked author bylines or editorial oversight signals
- 68% had minimal internal linking
- 61% showed scaled publishing with minimal QC

### Recovery Patterns That Worked
- Comprehensive topical coverage (rebuild as a topical fortress, not piecemeal edits)
- Adding/strengthening author bios + Person schema
- Pruning thin/duplicate content
- Adding original research, photos, data
- Reducing publishing velocity, raising per-article quality bar

### Patterns That DID NOT Work
- Surface-level edits to existing thin posts
- Adding generic AI-rewritten "freshness" passes
- Asking for forgiveness via Search Central forum

## 3. Author / Entity Signals — Implementation Checklist

**Why it matters:** 74% of HCU losers lacked bylines (Lily Ray). Author entity is one of the cheapest, fastest E-E-A-T fixes. [Source 4, 5]

### Per-article requirements
- [ ] Visible byline at top: "By [Full Name], [Credential]" with link to author page
- [ ] Author photo (real, not AI-generated)
- [ ] One-line credibility statement near byline ("12 years marketing local SMBs in Israel")
- [ ] Date published + last updated (both visible)
- [ ] Reviewer if applicable ("Reviewed by [Name, Credential]")

### Author archive page (one per author)
- [ ] Long-form bio (200+ words) — credentials, experience, focus areas
- [ ] Headshot
- [ ] sameAs links: LinkedIn, X, ORCID (if academic), personal site, GitHub if relevant
- [ ] List of published articles
- [ ] Contact method or social link

### Person schema (JSON-LD on author page AND embedded in Article schema)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Person",
  "name": "Adam K.",
  "url": "https://beamix.com/authors/adam",
  "image": "https://beamix.com/authors/adam.jpg",
  "jobTitle": "Founder, Beamix",
  "worksFor": { "@type": "Organization", "name": "Beamix" },
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.linkedin.com/in/...",
    "https://x.com/..."
  ],
  "knowsAbout": ["AI search visibility", "Generative engine optimization", "SMB marketing"],
  "alumniOf": "..."
}
```

### Article schema must include `author` as a Person reference
```json
"author": {
  "@type": "Person",
  "name": "Adam K.",
  "url": "https://beamix.com/authors/adam"
}
```

[Source 5, 6]

## 4. First-Hand Experience — Concrete Patterns

**The second 'E' (Experience) is what most AI-assisted content fails on.** It's also what Google explicitly weighted higher in 2024+ guidance.

### High-signal patterns
- "We tested X with Y for 30 days. Here's what happened." (with screenshots)
- "I scanned 50 SMB websites in March 2026 — these are the patterns I saw." (with anonymized data)
- Original photos with EXIF data (proves you were there)
- Named case studies: "When [Real Business Name] in Tel Aviv ran the agent…"
- Specific dates, prices, screenshots, error messages
- "I was wrong about [X] until [specific event/date]"
- Process narratives with timestamps ("On Tuesday morning at 9am we…")

### Low-signal patterns (avoid)
- "Many businesses find that…"
- "Studies show that…" (without citation)
- "It's important to note…"
- Round numbers ($100k, 50%) without source
- Generic stock photos

## 5. Trust Signals Ranked by Impact

| Rank | Signal | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | **HTTPS + clean domain reputation** | Non-negotiable, table stakes |
| 2 | **About page with real founder photos + bios** | The QRG explicitly evaluates this |
| 3 | **Contact page with real address, phone, email** | Especially for SMB-targeted brand |
| 4 | **Privacy Policy + Terms** | Linked in footer, not buried |
| 5 | **Author bios with credentials + sameAs schema** | See §3 |
| 6 | **External citations to authoritative sources** | Inline links to .gov, .edu, recognized publishers |
| 7 | **Third-party reviews / G2 / TrustPilot embeds** | When real |
| 8 | **No deceptive ads, no aggressive popups** | Direct QRG flag |
| 9 | **Original research / data on the site** | Big trust multiplier |
| 10 | **Consistent NAP (Name/Address/Phone) across web** | For local-relevant queries |

## 6. AI Content Policy — Current Stance + Caveats

**Official Google position (unchanged since Feb 2023, restated 2024):**
> "We focus on the quality of content, not how content is produced." — Danny Sullivan [Source 7]

**Spam policy update (March 2024):** Added "Scaled Content Abuse" — method-agnostic. Penalizes large amounts of unoriginal low-value content "no matter how it's created." [Source 7]

**Gary Illyes (2024):** Confirmed Google is internally discussing AI-experience claims (e.g., a review claiming firsthand use when AI-generated). No public policy yet.

**Practical reading:**
- AI-assisted content is fine. AI-only, scaled, low-value content is targeted.
- The question Google asks: "Would a person be satisfied if this was the only result?" — not "was this written by AI?"
- Disclosing AI use is recommended (transparency = trust signal) but not required for ranking.

**Beamix implication:** We can use AI heavily as a drafting tool. We must (a) human-edit every article, (b) add first-hand experience signals, (c) attach a real author with credentials, (d) include original data/observations. Then we are well within Google's guidance.

## 7. Beamix-Specific Application

### Author roster (build before publishing)
- **Founder voice (primary):** Adam K. — bio, photo, LinkedIn, "founder of Beamix, X years SMB marketing"
- **Specialist contributors:** Each agent persona = one expert author voice (Content Writer, FAQ Agent, etc.) — but the byline must be a real human, not an agent.
- **Optional:** External expert guest posts with their own Person schema

### Per-article E-E-A-T pre-publish checklist
- [ ] Real human byline + Person schema
- [ ] Last-updated date visible
- [ ] First-hand experience element (we tested / we scanned / we observed)
- [ ] At least 2 outbound links to authoritative sources (.gov, .edu, recognized publishers, primary research)
- [ ] At least 1 specific data point or original observation
- [ ] FAQPage schema if any Q&A blocks
- [ ] Article schema with author → Person link
- [ ] Internal links to ≥3 related Beamix articles
- [ ] Word count: ≥1,200 for blog pillar pieces, ≥600 for FAQ entries
- [ ] No "delve / tapestry / realm / landscape / navigate" (see HUMAN_SOUNDING_CONTENT.md)

### Trust signals on Framer site (one-time setup)
- [ ] About page with founder bio + photo + LinkedIn
- [ ] Contact page with real Israeli + global addresses
- [ ] Privacy + Terms (linked in footer)
- [ ] Customer logos / case studies (when available)
- [ ] Press / "as seen in" if applicable
- [ ] Author archive at /authors/[slug] for every author
