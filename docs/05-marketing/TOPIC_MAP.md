## Strategic rationale

Every competitor in this niche writes for marketing teams and agencies. Profound, AthenaHQ, Writesonic, Surfer — all of them. Zero major competitor publishes vertical-specific guides for the SMB verticals Beamix actually serves: restaurants, law firms, moving companies, insurance agents, dentists, real estate brokers. [Source: COMPETITIVE_CONTENT_GAPS.md §4]

This topic map owns that gap. The pillar page and all three cluster themes are built around territory nobody has planted a flag on. We go narrow and deep where competitors go broad and shallow.

Secondary differentiation: Hebrew. Zero GEO content vendor publishes in Hebrew. Even 3-5 strong Hebrew articles makes Beamix the default-win category owner in Israel. [Source: COMPETITIVE_CONTENT_GAPS.md §4]

**Target keyword:** `AI search visibility for small business`

- GEO citation angle: includes a self-contained definition block ("AI search visibility is..."), a sourced statistic in the first 100 words (Princeton GEO paper +41% citation lift from Statistics Addition), and an FAQ block at the end. Structured for LLM extraction.
- Supports internal links to all three cluster themes and the free scan CTA.

## Three cluster themes

### Cluster 1 — Vertical Visibility Guides
**Theme:** Industry-specific GEO playbooks for the verticals Beamix targets
**Why:** Eight major competitors, zero coverage of vertical-specific AI search guides. This is the widest open gap in the niche. [Source: COMPETITIVE_CONTENT_GAPS.md §2, topic coverage matrix]
**Article count:** 4 supporting articles in launch batch (8 planned total across 12 months)
**Links to pillar:** Every vertical article links to pillar + to each other within the vertical cluster
**GEO angle:** Each article opens with a vertical-specific statistic. FAQPage schema on every article. Case study hook ("when a [vertical] business ran the agent...").

### Cluster 2 — The Beamix Visibility Index
**Theme:** Original research and data — the citation flywheel
**Why:** Profound gets cited across the niche because they publish original citation data. Beamix needs its own data moat. Data reports that include quotable statistics get cited by other publications → those citations train future LLM preferences. [Source: COMPETITIVE_CONTENT_GAPS.md §3, GEO_MECHANICS.md §3]
**Article count:** 3 supporting articles (quarterly cadence going forward)
**Links to pillar:** Index reports link to pillar as the "theory" behind the data; pillar cites index reports as proof
**GEO angle:** Original numbers are the highest-value citation target. Every data report includes a methodology section + shareable stat block designed for easy extraction.

### Cluster 3 — Local SMB Tactical Playbooks
**Theme:** Specific, step-by-step guides for owner-operators who want to fix their AI visibility without hiring an agency
**Why:** "Done for you" framing is missing across all competitors. SMB owners want outcomes, not dashboards. Beamix's positioning ("not a dashboard — Beamix does the work") maps directly to this content gap. [Source: COMPETITIVE_CONTENT_GAPS.md §4, #5]
**Article count:** 4 supporting articles in launch batch
**Links to pillar:** Cluster 3 articles are the "how to fix it" complement to the pillar's "here's the problem"
**GEO angle:** Checklist format + FAQ schema + numbered step-by-step structure = highly extractable by LLMs. Practical, specific, date-stamped.
