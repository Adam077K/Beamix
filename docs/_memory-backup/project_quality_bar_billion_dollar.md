## The locked bar (Adam's words, 2026-04-25)

> "It should feel like expert level grade of a SaaS product. Something they never seen before and super super super super super professional and super super super expensive look. It should feel like a real company a billion dollar company designed this and created this product."

> "Not just like AI slope with just numbers and some regular charts. Something special like a product that a real designer is designing. A great team is thinking about every space, every product, every feature, every button, every space, every letter in the pages."

## What this means in practice

**Reference quality level (the bar):**
- Stripe Press / Stripe Dashboard
- Linear (post-2024 redesign)
- Apple (especially Vision Pro UI)
- Anthropic / Claude.ai
- Vercel Geist Design System
- Mercury Banking
- Notion (post-2023 redesign)
- Things 3 by Cultured Code
- Family.co / Basement / Tonik (studio-grade)

**NOT the bar:**
- Otterly / Profound / Peec (commodity GEO competitor look)
- PostHog (good but less polished than Stripe-tier)
- Generic Shadcn-template SaaS

## How to apply

For every design decision (component, page, animation, microcopy):
- Ask: "Does this feel like a $1B company shipped it, or a 2-week MVP?"
- Ask: "Is every pixel intentional, every space considered, every letter chosen?"
- Reject anything that reads as: AI-template, copy-paste, default-Shadcn, generic-charts, stock-icon, lorem-ipsum-feel.
- Animations and drawings are not decoration — they ARE the differentiation. Skipping them = looking like Otterly.
- Charts must NEVER be "regular charts" — every visualization needs intentional treatment (typography, spacing, color, motion).
- Spacing and rhythm are weapons — generic gap-4 is the enemy.
- Micro-decisions matter: letter-spacing, line-height, weight pairings, corner radii ratios, hover states, focus rings.

## /home is the most important surface

Adam: "This is the page most of the users will be the most." Quality bar applies to every page but `/home` is where the brand promise either lands or fails.

## How to apply this in agent briefs

Whenever briefing a design or frontend agent on Beamix work, include this principle. Do not allow "shipped, ship later" rationalizations on visual quality. The product can be incomplete (deferred features, missing pages) — but every shipped surface must clear the billion-dollar bar.
