## Rule

Do NOT include weeks / sprints / hours / days / time estimates in planning documents or recommendations to Adam.

**Why:** Adam's exact words (2026-04-25): *"You shouldn't worry about this. In your perspective, you're just planning with no timeline because I'm using an army of AI agents to do all of that. So time is not really important because I can ship what company ships in two months I can ship in three hours."*

**How to apply:**
- Plan by **scope, dependency order, and quality bar** — not by calendar.
- Phases describe *what comes first*, not *when*.
- Stop saying "Phase 0 ships in 4 days" or "Week 1: X, Week 2: Y." Use "first," "then," "after that," or just dependency arrows.
- Past-experience board recommendations citing "12-14 weeks" or "4-week MVP" → reframe as scope-only.
- Engineering risk and skill availability still matter — but they manifest as *what's possible* and *what's hard*, not as *deadlines*.

**Exception:** When Adam asks specifically "how long would this take?" — answer honestly with a range. Otherwise, don't volunteer time estimates.
